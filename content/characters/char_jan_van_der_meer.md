# Jan van der Meer

**Title:** CEO of GLC, Head of van der Meer Lithium Mining Dynasty

**Description (full):**

Jan van der Meer was born in 2018 as the youngest child of Pieter and Annabella van der Meer, growing up surrounded by privilege and opportunity. He moved to Las Flores with his family in 2030 at the age of 12, attending an exclusive private school where he met many influential people who would shape his future.

## Personality & Background

Jan was always more reserved than his older brother Alexander, preferring to observe rather than participate in the social scene that came with being a van der Meer. Instead, he focused on his studies and developing a deep understanding of the local culture and landscape — a passion that would serve him well as he took over the family business.

## Family Life

In 2045, at age 27, Jan married Maria Martinez, a stunningly beautiful woman from a prominent local family. They had three children together: Peter, Anna, and Lucas. As his father retired from GLC, Jan stepped into his role with grace and determination, guiding the company towards sustainable practices while maintaining strong relationships with both employees and community members.

## Brotherly Rivalry

Throughout his life, Jan struggled to live up to the reputation of his older brother Alexander — a man known for his extravagant lifestyle and reckless behavior. While Jan was reserved and focused on family, Alexander was charismatic but ultimately self-destructive. This contrast in personalities created tension between them until Alexander's public fall from grace forced him out of the company.

## Business Leadership

As the head of GLC, Jan oversaw a period of expansion and innovation for the lithium mining industry. He implemented cutting-edge environmental practices and invested heavily in community development projects, earning him widespread respect from both his employees and the local population.

## Personal Challenges

However, Jan's personal life was not without its challenges. His eldest son Peter came out as gay amidst controversy within their conservative social circle. Despite initial resistance from some family members, Jan ultimately supported his son's decision to marry his longtime partner in a lavish ceremony — an act that earned him even more respect among the younger generation.

Jan's daughter Anna followed her father into the family business while also pursuing an activist career advocating for LGBTQ+ rights. She married a Dutch cousin, further solidifying the van der Meer family's international connections.

Tragically, Jan's youngest child Lucas struggled with drug addiction and mental health issues stemming from childhood trauma. He ended up in a psychiatric institution after several public incidents, forcing his parents to keep his condition hidden to protect the family's reputation.

## Legacy and Death

On January 15th, 2060, at age 41, Jan died suddenly of a heart attack while on a business trip abroad. His wife Maria was devastated by the loss and began to retreat into her own world, struggling with dementia that clouded her memories of Old Las Flores. In the wake of their father's death, Peter, Anna, and Lucas stepped in to manage both their mother's care and the family business.

Jan van der Meer's legacy lives on through his children's leadership and the sustainable practices he implemented at GLC. His ability to balance tradition with progress, and family loyalty with business acumen, made him one of the most respected figures in Las Flores' mining industry.
