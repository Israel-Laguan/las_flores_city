# Anna van der Meer

> Tags: `#figure` `#npc` `#van_der_meer` `#ceo` `#lgbtq` `#environmentalism` `#city_council`

## Overview

**Born:** ~2041  
**Age:** 36 in 2077 (game present)  
**Family:** Daughter of Jan and Maria van der Meer; sister of Peter; wife of Han Jr.; no children  
**Role:** Van der Meer Industries CEO, social advocate, later Las Flores City Council member  
**Faction:** `van_der_meer`  
**Personality:** `empathetic_pragmatic_reformer`

Anna van der Meer is the only daughter of Jan and Maria van der Meer, born into one of the wealthiest and most influential families in South America. Raised amid the grandeur and political complexity of Old Las Flores, Anna was shaped by a fierce sense of social responsibility that would define her life's work.

---

## Background

Anna's early years were marked by privilege and opportunity. She attended elite schools and learned the family business from the inside by watching her father navigate mining, international investment, and local politics. Despite her upbringing, Anna remained empathetic toward marginalized communities and sensitive to the damage caused by extractive wealth. She learned to move through elite circles with grace, but never lost her conscience.

---

## Jan's Death and Rise to CEO

When Jan van der Meer died suddenly in 2060, Anna was 19 years old. The loss was devastating, but she inherited control of Van der Meer Industries through a family trust. During her early years as the nominal head, day-to-day operations were managed by experienced board members while Anna completed her education and prepared for leadership.

---

## Activism

Anna's activism began in her late twenties, when she organized protests against environmental degradation caused by Van der Meer Industries' mining operations. Her public criticism of her own family's business earned her both praise and notoriety. She also became known for socially conscious photography, including work focused on inequality and the lives of communities excluded from the city's wealth. This artistic advocacy gave her a public platform separate from the family name.

---

## Marriage

In her early 30s, around 2072, Anna married Han Jr., the son of a Dutch cousin who had moved to Las Flores to work for the family business. The marriage strengthened ties between the Van der Meer family and its European connections. Anna and Han Jr. had no children, but they remained devoted partners throughout their lives.

---

## LGBTQ+ Advocacy

Anna's advocacy for LGBTQ+ rights intensified after her brother Peter came out as gay. She fiercely defended him and used her social platforms to raise awareness about the struggles faced by the LGBTQ+ community in Las Flores. She co-founded a nonprofit focused on empowering LGBTQ+ youth in Las Flores and served as its president for more than two decades. Her work helped normalize public support for queer rights within the conservative mining dynasty.

---

## Van der Meer Industries

After completing her education and gaining experience, Anna took active control of Van der Meer Industries. She was determined to honor her father's legacy while modernizing the company for a changing world. Her tenure included bold initiatives to reduce environmental impact, improve worker conditions, and push the company toward a more responsible public posture. Some board members resisted, citing profit concerns, but Anna's conviction and family influence carried the day.

---

## Family & Caregiving

As Maria's dementia worsened after 2071, Anna struggled to balance her work as CEO with her responsibilities as a daughter and caregiver. She considered hiring a full-time nurse but hesitated, worried that strangers would not provide the same level of care and dignity her mother deserved. Instead, Anna relied heavily on Dirk, the loyal housekeeper who had served the family since before Anna's birth. Her days were filled with corporate meetings, strategy, and advocacy, while many nights were spent sitting at her mother's bedside.

---

## Political Career

Anna entered politics and won a seat on the Las Flores City Council in 2078. She used the platform to advocate for environmental protection, expanded civil rights, education, community investment, and responsible development. Conservative council members opposed her often, but Anna remained an unapologetic progressive voice.

---

## Appearance

Anna is 5'7, with an athletic, toned build shaped by an active lifestyle and love of outdoor pursuits. She has dark brown hair, usually styled in a practical but elegant manner, and deep blue eyes. Her style shifts between tailored suits for business and politics, and casual but refined clothing for activism and social events.

---

## Personality

Anna is empathetic and a strong listener, especially with constituents and vulnerable communities. She is also a skilled mediator who can navigate complex family, corporate, and political landscapes with patience and resolve. Her defining traits are social conviction, discipline, loyalty to family, and an unwavering commitment to environmental and LGBTQ+ justice.

---

## Legacy

Anna van der Meer left behind a legacy of compassion, reform, and public courage. She co-founded an LGBTQ+ youth nonprofit, modernized Van der Meer Industries as CEO, fought for environmental and workers' rights, and helped redefine what it meant to be a public leader in Las Flores. Her life remains a touchstone for future generations of Las Flores activists who believe elite power can be forced into public service.
