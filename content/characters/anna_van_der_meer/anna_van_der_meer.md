# Anna van der Meer

> Tags: `#figure` `#npc` `#van_der_meer` `#ceo` `#lgbtq` `#environmentalism` `#city_council`

## Overview

**Age:** 35 in 2025; 69 in 2059; 86 in 2077  
**Family:** Daughter of Jan and Maria van der Meer; sister of Peter; wife of Han Jr.  
**Role:** Van der Meer Industries CEO, social advocate, later Las Flores City Council member  
**Faction:** `van_der_meer`  
**Personality:** `empathetic_pragmatic_reformer`

Anna van der Meer is the only daughter of Jan and Maria van der Meer, born into one of the wealthiest and most influential families in South America. Raised amid the grandeur and political complexity of Old Las Flores society, she became independent, outspoken, assertive, and deeply conscious of the responsibility attached to family power.

---

## Background

Anna's early years were marked by privilege and opportunity. She attended elite schools and learned the family business from the inside by watching her father navigate mining, international investment, and Old Las Flores society. Her parents instilled in her a commitment to family, public service, and community responsibility.

Despite her upbringing, Anna remained empathetic toward marginalized communities and sensitive to the damage caused by extractive wealth. She learned to move through elite circles with grace, but she never fully accepted their indifference to inequality.

---

## Activism

Anna's activism began young, when she organized protests against environmental degradation caused by Van der Meer Industries' mining operations. Her public criticism of her own family's business earned admiration from reformers and criticism from Las Flores' elite, but she remained undeterred.

She also became known for socially conscious photography, including work focused on inequality and the lives of communities excluded from the city's wealth. This artistic advocacy gave her a public voice before she became a formal business or political leader.

---

## Marriage

In her early 30s, around 2044, Anna married Han Jr., the son of a Dutch cousin who had moved to Las Flores to work for the family business. The marriage strengthened ties between the Van der Meer clan and its European relatives, but it also allowed Anna to maintain independence within a strategic family union.

Anna and Han Jr. had no children, but they remained devoted partners throughout their lives.

---

## LGBTQ+ Advocacy

Anna's advocacy for LGBTQ+ rights intensified after her brother Peter came out as gay. She fiercely defended him and used her social platforms to raise awareness about the struggles faced by the LGBTQ+ community. Her stance caused controversy in conservative Las Flores circles, but it also earned respect from many younger activists and marginalized residents.

She co-founded a nonprofit focused on empowering LGBTQ+ youth in Las Flores and served as its president for more than two decades. Her work helped normalize public support for queer rights within one of the city's most powerful families.

---

## Van der Meer Industries

After Jan's sudden death in 2060, Anna became CEO of Van der Meer Industries. She was determined to honor her father's legacy while modernizing the company for a changing world.

Her tenure included bold initiatives to reduce environmental impact, improve worker conditions, and push the company toward a more responsible public posture. Some board members resisted, citing profitability and tradition, but Anna navigated the internal politics with strategic discipline and unwavering conviction.

---

## Family & Caregiving

As Maria's dementia worsened after 2071, Anna struggled to balance her work as CEO with her responsibilities as a daughter and caregiver. She considered hiring a full-time nurse but hesitated, worried it might upset family members or be seen as an admission that she could not care for Maria herself.

Instead, Anna relied heavily on Dirk, the loyal housekeeper who had served the family since before Anna's birth. Her days were filled with corporate meetings, strategy, and advocacy, while many nights were spent managing the emotional toll of Maria's decline.

---

## Political Career

In later years, Anna entered politics and won a seat on the Las Flores City Council in 2078. She used the platform to advocate for environmental protection, expanded civil rights, education, community development, and stronger protections for marginalized groups.

Conservative council members opposed her often, but Anna remained an unapologetic progressive voice until her death in 2085.

---

## Appearance

Anna is 5'7, with an athletic, toned build shaped by an active lifestyle and love of outdoor pursuits. She has dark brown hair, usually styled in a practical but elegant manner, and deep blue eyes that reflect both willpower and empathy. Her strong jawline, high cheekbones, and determined expression give her a commanding presence.

Her style shifts between tailored suits for business and politics, and casual but refined clothing for activism and social events.

---

## Personality

Anna is empathetic and a strong listener, especially with constituents and vulnerable communities. She is also a skilled mediator who can navigate complex family, corporate, and political landscapes. Beneath her warm public exterior is a shrewd and pragmatic strategist capable of difficult decisions.

Her defining traits are social conviction, discipline, loyalty to family, and an unwavering commitment to environmental and LGBTQ+ justice.

---

## Legacy

Anna van der Meer left behind a legacy of compassion, reform, and public courage. She co-founded an LGBTQ+ youth nonprofit, modernized Van der Meer Industries as CEO, fought for environmental and worker reforms, and used her City Council seat to champion progressive policies.

Her life remains a touchstone for future generations of Las Flores activists who believe elite power can be forced into public service.
