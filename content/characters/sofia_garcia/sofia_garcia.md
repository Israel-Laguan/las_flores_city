# Sofia Garcia

**Title:** Nurse, Coastal Clinic Volunteer, Alex's Moral Compass

**Physical Description:**
- Mid-30s: tired but warm eyes, dark hair tied back in practical ponytail
- Always wears blue-flower embroidered bandage on left wrist (gift from Alejandro)
- Carries weathered thermos with herbal tea, medical kit with extra bandages
- Wears faded blue nursing scrubs, sturdy shoes for walking between clinics

**Description (full):**

Sofia Garcia is the middle child of Alejandro and Rosa, born ~2058. Unlike her siblings, she stayed close to home after Rosa's death, training as a nurse in the coastal towns affected by lithium contamination. She witnessed firsthand how the poisoned water sickened her neighbors, which drove her to specialize in respiratory treatments and herbal medicine.

After Alex left for Las Flores with the Van der Meer scholarship, Sofia became Alejandro's primary emotional support. She visits the flower fields every two weeks, bringing medical supplies and news from the outside world. When Alex's letters stopped coming regularly after the movement began, Sofia kept Alejandro calm, telling him: "Alex is building something bigger than us. We have to trust them."

Sofia was in the coastal clinic when the 2077 broadcast aired. She heard about Alex's sacrifice from a patient who had seen the news. She didn't cry—she just clutched the last letter Alex had sent her (the one saying "Tell Papa I'm trying to build something better") and continued treating her patient. That night, she embroidered a blue flower onto a bandage and has worn it every day since.

In the post-2077 landscape, Sofia divides her time between the Mar del Sol Hospital and mobile clinics serving refugee camps. She's known for never turning away a patient, even when supplies run low. Travelers often seek her out for medical aid and news from the Garcia family.

**Player Encounter Locations & Dialogue Hooks:**

1. **Mar del Sol Hospital Entrance** (Shift change: 6 AM or 6 PM)
   - *Trigger:* Player waits outside during shift change
   - *Sofia behavior:* Emerges from hospital doors, pauses to check watch, scans for anyone unusual. Wears blue-flower bandage visibly. Carries thermos.
   - *Dialogue options:*
     - "You're Alex's sister?" → "I was. Now I'm just someone who remembers him. What do you need?"
     - "The blue flower—" → "Alejandro taught us: blue means honest work. Alex wore one every day. I wear one for every patient I can't save."
     - "I have a letter..." → [Takes letter carefully] "This handwriting... Where did you find this? I thought all his letters were lost in the crackdown."
   - *Reward:* Blue-flower embroidered bandage (healing bonus for 60 seconds)

2. **Mobile Clinic Route** (Sector 7B, near refugee camp)
   - *Trigger:* Player helps refugees or defends clinic from raiders
   - *Sofia behavior:* Focuses on treating wounded, instructs volunteers efficiently. Speaks in calm, measured tones even under pressure.
   - *Dialogue options:
     - "Need help?" → "Grab those bandages. No—blue ones, not white. Color coding matters when you're treating twenty people at once."
     - "Your brother saved—" → "He saved the city. I just try to save people one at a time. Same goal, smaller scale."
     - "Why do you stay?" → "Because someone has to. Because Alex believed in building instead of burning. Because my patients need me tomorrow."
   - *Reward:* Herbal medicine bundle (unlock: "Nurse's Wisdom" - crafting recipe)

3. **Garcia Memorial Plot** (Visiting Alejandro, if player has unlocked location)
   - *Trigger:* Player visits plot on same day as Sofia's bi-weekly visit
   - *Sofia behavior:* Kneels at white flower section, places fresh bandages on irrigation stone, speaks quietly to Alejandro. Turns to player with composed but sad expression.
   - *Dialogue options:
     - "You visit often?" → "Every two weeks. Alejandro needs to see someone who remembers Alex like he does. The other patients—they know the name but not the person."
     - "He talked about me?" → "Every time. 'Sofia's the brave one,' he'd say. 'She stayed when I ran. She heals when I fight.' He was proud of all of us."
