# Alejandro Garcia

**Title:** Flower Farmer, Construction Worker, Memorial Keeper

**Tall and stooped from years of field work, hands permanently stained with soil and lithium residue. Salt-and-pepper beard, deep-set eyes that constantly scan for danger. Wears patched work clothes, boots caked with dried clay from the poisoned fields. Always carries a small cloth pouch containing three pressed flowers—one for each child.

**Description (full):**

Born ~2030, Alejandro inherited the Garcia family's flower fields near the Río de las Flores. He married Rosa around 2050, and together they raised three children: Alex (born ~2055, the eldest), Sofia (born ~2058), and Elena (born ~2061). When lithium contamination from Minera Estrella's operations reached their fields, Rosa fell ill first—her respiratory system failing by 2068. The loss broke Alejandro, but he channeled his pain into two things: nurturing the crops that had sustained his family for generations and teaching his children life skills.

For Alex, he maintained strict routines around farming and homework, teaching him to read soil patterns and sketch structural designs in the dirt. For Sofia, who preferred the company of strangers, he insisted on strict medical hygiene in their home and taught her basic wound care. For Elena, who grew restless, he let her tend special 'legacy blooms'—white night-blooming cereus that only grew in the least contaminated corners of their fields.

When Alex took the Van der Meer scholarship to Las Flores at age 20, Alejandro initially tried to keep her close but eventually realized separate growth was necessary. He continued the flower fields as a living memorial, harvesting one special bloom each month to plant in La Flor de la Gloria Park. Sofia became a nurse in the coastal towns, often sending medical supplies to Alejandro's fields. Elena worked in resistance cells but kept visiting to harvest their joint legacy project: a vertical hydroponic farm in Sector Norte that grew both food and medicinal herbs.

After Alex's sacrifice in 2077, Alejandro transformed his grief into purpose. He maintains the Garcia Memorial Plot—a section of his fields where he grows only white flowers (symbolizing purity and remembrance) and has carved Alex's name into the irrigation stone. He speaks with travelers about the dangers of corporate negligence, sharing Alex's story as both warning and inspiration. Though his hands tremble from lithium exposure, he still works the fields daily, believing that tending life is the best resistance against death.

## Legacy & Emotional Core

Alejandro maintains that Alex's death was not sacrifice but revelation: *"He didn't die to prove he could build. He died to show we could build anew."* His memorial plot contains Alex's original blueprint fragments that only those who "listen to the dirt" can decipher. He believes Alex's final words—*"Tell Papa I'm trying to build something better"*—mean rebuilding systems, not just structures.

The three family members embody Alex's legacy in different ways:
- **Sofia:** He writes letters & tends to refugees: "Alex was about small acts of hope. I save lives; he saved systems."
- **Elena:** Challenges Alejandro's idealism: "You say rebuild? I say rebuild *differently*. Alex would've wanted the towers to grow food, not flowers."
- **Player:** Becomes the vessel through which Alex's legacy manifests: Eastern Bloc warships repurposed into housing fleets because "Alex always knew how to turn scrap into salvation."

He still tends the plot where he and Alex would sit, sketching buildings in the dirt. Now he carries a waterproof sketchbook filled with Alex's old plans. When players complete key quests involving systemic change, he adds those events to Alex's blueprint fragments, whispering *"He would've wanted this system too."* When repairs complete, he places one blue-flower pin on the irrigation stone—Alex's original marker for "a project demanding leadership and integrity."

Alex's legacy isn't a single monument but a living conversation: 
- Alejandro tends flowers as memorial 
- Sofia tends to the sick as ministry 
- Elena builds upward food systems as resistance 
- Player inherits Alex's final mantle

**Full Dialogue Trigger: "Tell Papa I'm trying to build something better"**
- Appears when player completes 3+ systemic change quests
- Alejandro/testament: *"He meant 'don't fix just one thing—fix how the whole city thinks.'"
- Sofia/testament: *"He meant 'keep healing people when governments fail to do so.'"
- Elena/testament: *"He meant 'don't build structures—build the people who build them.' Now come build with me."

This creates a player-driven system where Alex's legacy lives through the family's distinct paths, with meaningful connections to gameplay mechanics of architecture, healthcare, and infrastructure rebuilding.
