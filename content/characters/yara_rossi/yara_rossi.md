## Overview

Yara Rossi was a successful businesswoman who became an environmental activist after meeting Evelyn Ruthenberg. Born in Ecuador in 2019, she moved to Las Flores with her family in 2024 at age 5, when the port burst into activity amid the lithium mine's opening. She built a successful import-export firm specializing in sustainable goods long before "green" became a buzzword, driven by her grandmother's teachings that "true wealth is measured in integrity, not just profit." She used her wealth, influence, and charisma to champion the cause of justice for the Great Lithium Leak victims.

## Family Background

Yara's father was a dockworker at the Port of Las Flores. He was killed in a suspicious "accident" in 2033 when Yara was 14, after attempting to report irregular port movements to the police. His death—officially ruled an industrial mishap but suspected to be Minería Estrella-sponsored silencing—became an unspoken specter in Yara's life, reinforcing her deep distrust of corporate and institutional corruption and fueling her determination to fight for justice. Her mother later confided that Minería Estrella had made her father disappear, though no proof could be found. This personal loss transformed quiet observation into active resistance.

Her grandmother, who raised her with stories of ancestors' past injustices, taught her resilience: "True wealth is measured in integrity, not just profit." Her mother's strength and quiet suffering also shaped Yara's worldview. Witnessing firsthand how political favors and unchecked corporate greed eroded Las Flores' soul—particularly the impact on marginalized communities and the environment—festered within her from an early age.

## Early Life & Education (2019–2040)

Born in Ecuador in 2019, Yara moved to Las Flores in 2024 as the lithium mine opened and the port expanded. She attended a small local school near the port (2024–2029), then middle school in Bahia de Las Flores (2029–2034). The death of her father in 2033, when she was 14, marked a turning point. She graduated from high school in 2038 with a strong academic record and a clear vision to build her own business, earning a scholarship to Universidad Internacional to study business and entrepreneurship (2038–2040), followed by a postgraduate degree at Universidad del Valle specializing in sustainable business practices (2040).

During 2037–2042, she formed a group of young entrepreneurs and established several successful ventures, leading initiatives as a skilled seller, communicator, and leader.

## Entrepreneurial Career (2040–2045)

Yara started her import-export firm specializing in sustainable goods in 2042, driven by a deep-seated belief in ethical practices and fair trade. Her ascent wasn't fueled by ruthless ambition, but by values ingrained by her grandmother. While she cultivated an image of unflappable success, a current of unease ran beneath: witnessing how political favors and corporate greed eroded Las Flores.

In 2043, one of her companies went bankrupt. Yara blamed the LW Group for monopolistic practices and corruption. By 2045, she had expanded her business operations again, leveraging her success to support local communities and environmental initiatives.

## Personal Life

Yara's personal life included a painful chapter with her partner Gabriel. After a fertility diagnosis shattered her hopes of starting a family, she discovered his infidelity. Their brutal breakup in 2044—captured in a raw night of confrontation and tears—left her questioning her priorities and ultimately redirecting her energy into activism and community building.

## Activism & Leadership (2043–2052)

From 2043 to 2052, Yara continued building her business empire while becoming increasingly aware of the negative impact of corporate greed. In 2045, she began quietly supporting various environmental and social justice causes, using her resources and influence to back grassroots movements.

After the Great Lithium Leak in 2052, Yara sponsored and led protest initiatives against Minería Estrella and the LW Group. In 2053, she supported local investigative journalism that uncovered the extent of environmental damage and corruption linked to Minería Estrella. In 2055, the trial of Minería Estrella executives began; Yara provided financial and strategic support to those seeking to hold the company accountable.

In 2057, despite evidence of widespread corruption and negligence, Minería Estrella was acquitted on most charges due to technicalities and legal loopholes. Yara's resolve to seek justice deepened.

## Alliance with Evelyn Ruthenberg (February 2058)

Their first encounter in February 2058 was in a nightclub. Evelyn, then a guarded figure nursing her own wounds, had been handing off evidence to sources. Yara, there to escape the daily grind, struck up a conversation. When the topic turned to Minería Estrella, Evelyn's guardedness melted into passionate resolve. "I will expose those bastards with the truth," she told Yara. That night, Yara recognized a kindred spirit. A week later, Yara invited Evelyn to her apartment, where she introduced her to Dr. Wei Zhang, a brilliant chemist from Universidad del Valle trusted by Yara for years. Evelyn shared her evidence, and Yara committed her resources and strategic acumen to the cause.

## Restoration Project (February 2058–July 2059)

Together with Evelyn and Wei Zhang, they devised a comprehensive phytoremediation and habitat restoration plan. Yara rallied local support, secured permits, and raised funds through international investors and environmental organizations. The project began in January 2059.

She maintained her public activism: in 2054 she marched with university students in Old Las Flores, holding a handmade placard and defending their right to protest. Her direct, no-nonsense approach made her a trusted figure among grassroots activists and strategic leaders alike.

## Public Announcement (May 2059)

As the restoration project showed promising results, Yara publicly announced her intention to reopen the case against Minera Estrella based on new evidence. She urged the international community to support their efforts.

## Anonymous Whistleblower (June 2059)

An anonymous whistleblower sent Yara the work of Liu Fang with more internal proofs, including footage of the night of the pipe explosion showing no rain, and original auditor reports about problems with the containers. This evidence gave Yara the confidence to move forward with reopening the case.

## Assassination (July 14, 2059)

Yara was assassinated while returning from a meeting with investors who pledged additional support for the restoration project. Her murder remains unsolved, but many suspect Minería Estrella's involvement. The evidence memory card was taken from her body.

## Legacy

Yara's death galvanized the environmental movement in Las Flores. Her restoration project continued under Evelyn's leadership, and her story became a symbol of resistance against corporate impunity.
