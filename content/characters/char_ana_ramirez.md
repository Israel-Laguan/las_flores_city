# Ana Ramirez

**Title (full):** Environmental Activist, Indigenous Rights Advocate, Founder of La Coalición por la Justicia Social (CJS)

**Description (full):**

Ana Ramirez is a 32-year-old passionate indigenous political leader who heads La Coalición por la Justicia Social (CJS). Born into a family of indigenous activists in Las Flores' rural outskirts, she witnessed firsthand the impact of mining activities on native communities and their ancestral lands from an early age. This inspired her to pursue a degree in environmental science and dedicate herself to protecting both the environment and indigenous rights.

As the founder of CJS, Ana crafts effective strategies for advocacy and education, working closely with progressive politicians and grassroots initiatives. She supports Yara Rossi's restoration efforts, seeing them as a means of mitigating mining damage and fostering sustainable development. Ana's deep commitment to her cause drives her to take bold stances against powerful interests, always centering the experiences of native communities.

Her leadership style is grounded in humility, always prioritizing the voices of the indigenous communities she serves. She speaks eloquently about the intersectionality of environmental justice and indigenous rights, prefers nonviolent direct action and grassroots organizing, and maintains a strong connection to her cultural heritage by incorporating traditional practices into CJS's advocacy work.
