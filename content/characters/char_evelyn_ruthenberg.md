# Evelyn Ruthenberg

**Title (full):** Whistleblower & Shadow Investigator, Former Luz del Rio Engineer

**Description (full):**

Evelyn Ruthenberg is a former Luz del Rio engineer who became a whistleblower and covert investigator after discovering the Great Lithium Leak cover-up. Graduating from Universidad Nacional de Las Flores with an engineering degree in early 2051, she secured a position at Luz del Rio, a small electric company serving the mining and industrial sectors.

## The Great Lithium Leak and Discovery

In February 2052, a catastrophic failure of the tailings dam at Minería Estrella's lithium mine led to a massive spill that contaminated nearby rivers, killing hundreds in downstream communities. By March 2052, Governor Ricardo Rivas, City Mayor Alfonso Vega, and Minería Estrella officials issued public statements downplaying the disaster and imposing restrictions on independent reporting.

## Meeting Karla and Finding Evidence

In February 2053, Evelyn met Karla, a local farmer whose land had been contaminated by the lithium leak. Karla provided studies demonstrating lithium affecting the Carinata plantation a year before the Great Leak. By September 2053, Evelyn discovered that Karla's sister, Nubia, had been killed. She found a small box containing evidence of the contamination, including a copy of the study from biologist Cecilia Perez and internal emails between Minería Estrella executives discussing dam structural issues before the leak. In October 2053, Evelyn investigated further but decided not to come forward with the evidence, fearing the consequences.

## The Trial and Attack

In January 2055, the trial of Minería Estrella executives began in a special tribunal established by the Free City's constitution. Governor Ricardo Rivas and key political allies, including Senator Ricardo Chen, were implicated in the scandal. In March 2055, Evelyn attempted to present the evidence she had found but was attacked by gang members. She fled the city and took refuge in her family home in the South District Valley.

## Acquittal and Personal Loss

In January 2057, despite evidence of widespread corruption and negligence, Minería Estrella was acquitted on most charges due to technicalities and legal loopholes. The company agreed to a modest fine and promised improved environmental standards. In February 2057, Governor Rafael Sáenz decided not to seek re-election amid mounting pressure. In March 2057, Minería Estrella demanded payment from Luz del Rio, claiming a faulty electrical component caused the leak. Evelyn tried to send the evidence anonymously to the defense team of Luz del Rio but discovered later that the evidence was not used in the trial.

## Return and Continued Fight

On January 1, 2058, Evelyn boarded a bus to the city, determined to expose the truth and seek justice for her community and her mother, who had died from complications related to the lithium contamination. She carried with her the evidence she had been too afraid to share before, now fueled by a resolve born of personal loss and a desire for justice.

After her return, Evelyn allied with Yara Rossi and Dr. Wei Zhang to build a phytoremediation restoration plan. Simultaneously, she cultivated a covert intelligence network, tracking corrupt officials and manipulating informants. When Yara was assassinated on July 14, 2059, Evelyn identified the body in the morgue and vowed to continue fighting in both the light and the shadows.
