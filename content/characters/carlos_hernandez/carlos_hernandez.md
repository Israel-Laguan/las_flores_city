# Carlos Hernandez

>
> **Born:** ~1975 (Las Flores)
> **Role:** Mayor of Old Las Flores (2000–2034), Mayor of City District (2034–2038)
> **Status:** Retired (as of 2038)

## Overview

Carlos Hernandez was the dominant political figure in Las Flores during its transformation from a small mining town into a global lithium hub. Serving as mayor for nearly four decades, he navigated the city through the lithium boom, the arrival of Chinese and Dutch mining interests, and the controversial creation of the Free City. His political career ended in 2038 with a reputation as a "builder," though his tenure was later overshadowed by corruption allegations and the environmental catastrophe of 2052.

## Background & Early Career

Carlos Hernandez began his mayoral career in 2000 at approximately age 25, presiding over a quiet fishing and mining town of around 5,000 residents. His youthful energy and vision for unity among the diverse communities earned him significant support from both locals and the growing Chinese population.

He was re-elected multiple times, maintaining power through the town's dramatic transformation. Early in his career, Hernandez struggled with a gambling addiction — a secret he managed to keep hidden with the help of close friends and allies who helped him overcome it. This experience instilled in him a deep empathy for those battling personal demons, a trait that would define his political style.

## The Lithium Boom and CLM Deal (2024–2026)

The 2023 discovery of massive lithium reserves transformed Las Flores. Hernandez recognized an unprecedented opportunity, but his decision-making was influenced by personal vulnerabilities — his past gambling struggles and rumors about his ties to the Chinese community created a complex web of motivations.

### 2025–2026: Negotiating the "Historic Opportunity"

By 2025, during his re-election campaign, Hernandez was firmly positioned as the architect of Las Flores' economic future. His team identified the lithium deposits as the cornerstone of that future.

**December 2024:** China Lithium & Metals (CLM) representatives reached out to Hernandez's campaign, recognizing his progressive stance and vision for economic development.

**March 2025:** CLM officially presented their interest in the lithium deposits to the municipal government, offering advanced mining technology, significant capital investment, and promises of job creation. Behind closed doors, high-level discussions began between CLM executives and Hernandez's advisers. The CLM team knew how to appeal to Hernandez's ambitions and his need for a political win.

**Mid-2025:** Hernandez's team drafted regulations and concessions. Publicly, Carlos framed this initiative as a "historic opportunity" for Las Flores, emphasizing job creation and modernization.

**July–August 2025:** Intensive lobbying by CLM swayed key city council members, promising investment in local projects and community welfare programs. Amid public debate and some opposition from environmentalists and local business groups, the council approved the deal.

**September 2025:** The deal was formally signed. CLM gained full control over lithium operations with generous tax incentives and regulatory exemptions. Local media portrayed it as a landmark achievement for Mayor Hernandez.

Hernandez leveraged the momentum to launch community improvement initiatives funded by initial investments, bolstering his image ahead of the 2026 re-election. He won comfortably, but whispers about the nature of his relationship with Chinese business interests had already begun circulating.

- **2026**: Mining concession formally approved; Hernandez re-elected with strong Chinese backing.
- **2028**: Groundbreaking ceremony for Minera Estrella's mine, attended by Hernandez and Chinese Ambassador Li Wei.
- **2030**: First lithium shipment departed; Hernandez declared a state of emergency to request central government infrastructure funding.
- **2033**: The Dutch mining consortium **Van der Meer** (subsidiary of Global Lithium Corp) discovered an additional lithium vein, shaking the Chinese monopoly.

## Scandals and Secrets

Despite his popularity, rumors about Carlos persisted throughout his career:

- **Chinese Ties:** Whispers of illicit campaign contributions and favors exchanged for political support circulated during his later terms. While no concrete evidence ever surfaced, the allegations cast a shadow over his otherwise illustrious career.
- **Extramarital Affair:** It was rumored that Carlos had an affair with a local journalist during his mayoral tenure. Never confirmed, but the gossip strained his relationship with his wife and children for a time.
- **Misappropriation Allegations (2058):** Retrospective allegations surfaced in 2058 regarding the misappropriation of mayoral-era city funds for personal use. An investigation cleared him of wrongdoing, but whispers persisted that he had used his influence to quash the inquiry. These claims related to decisions made during his tenure, not continued official activity after 2038.
- **Luxury Lifestyle:** An open secret among the city's elite, Carlos had a penchant for expensive gifts and luxurious vacations, leading to speculation about the sources of his wealth and the favors he might be trading for such perks.

## The Free City Maneuver (2033–2034)

By 2033, Hernandez's traditional political base in Old Las Flores had eroded. The **Popular Party of Las Flores (PLF)** gained traction among residents frustrated by rapid changes and growing inequality. Hernandez recognized he could not win re-election in the old city.

When the Free City proposal emerged, led by **Juan Pablo Ramos** and **Pieter van der Meer**, Hernandez accepted an offer to become the first **Mayor of the City District** — the administrative core of the new metropolis. The city was only **60% complete** when he took office in 2034: streets were laid but largely empty, buildings stood unfinished, and the population was a fraction of what it would become.

Rather than retiring in defeat, Hernandez reframed his departure from Old Las Flores as a "promotion" — ascending to lead the new financial and political center. This allowed him to oversee the city's completion and retire with prestige.

## Governance of the City District (2034–2038)

Carlos Hernandez served as Mayor of the City District from 2034 to 2038, a period he characterized as "transition and construction." During this time:

- He supervised the final phases of infrastructure development.
- He maintained a working relationship with Governor Ramos, though the two operated in separate spheres (district vs. regional governance).
- He avoided the major social crises that erupted after 2038, when the population surpassed 500,000 and labor tensions escalated.

## Retirement and Legacy

Hernandez retired from public office in 2038, stepping down before the **"Estado de Excepción"** declared by Governor Ramos in response to social unrest. His retirement was marked by public praise for his role as a "constructor of the Free City," allowing him to exit politics with a favorable public reputation.

However, his legacy remained complicated:
- He was deeply influential in the CLM/Minera Estrella deal that set the stage for the 2052 disaster.
- Persistent rumors of Chinese business ties, misappropriation, and quid-pro-quo arrangements followed him into retirement.
- The corruption scandals involving his son, **Carlos Alberto Hernandez**, whose 2038 marriage to **Elisabeth van der Meer** and entanglement in ELU graft further tarnished the family name.

In **2059**, at age 84, Hernandez was still visible in the community — attending city council meetings and community events, advocating for unity and progress. By **2077**, at 102 years old, he had fully retired but remained a significant figure, mentoring young leaders and engaging in philanthropy.
