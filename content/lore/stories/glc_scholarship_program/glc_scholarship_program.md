# Dutch Firm Launches Scholarship Program to Combat Gang Violence in Old Las Flores

> Tags: `#story` `#education` `#scholarship` `#gang_violence` `#van_der_meer`
>
> **Author:** Pedro Alvarez, Education Reporter (El Informador)

## Overview

Global Lithium Corp (GLC) launched **"Futuro Brillante"** (Bright Future), an ambitious scholarship program supporting talented students from Old Las Flores to pursue higher education and combat gang violence through opportunity.

---

## Program Details

- **Full scholarships** for high school seniors from Old Las Flores
- **Mentorship opportunities** with GLC professionals
- **Internships** in the lithium industry providing valuable work experience
- **Priority** for applicants from families with limited financial resources and demonstrated commitment to community service

### Selection Criteria
- Academic excellence
- Leadership potential
- Ability to overcome adversity

### CEO Statement
> "We believe that investing in the next generation is key to creating a brighter future for all residents of Las Flores. By providing these young people with the tools they need to succeed, we can help break the cycle of poverty and violence that plagues their community." — **Jan van Dijk**, CEO of Global Lithium Corp

## Community Impact

- **Old Las Flores demographics:** Over 70% of residents live below the poverty line
- **School principal Luis Rodriguez:** "For too long, they've been overlooked and underserved by the city's educational system. With GLC's support, we can finally give them the chance they deserve."

## Broader GLC Commitment

Beyond scholarships, GLC pledged to:
- Work with local schools and community organizations in Old Las Flores
- Develop targeted educational programming to prevent youth involvement in gang activity
- Foster a more peaceful and prosperous future through investment in young people
