# El Contagio: How the Río de las Flores Crisis is Impacting Las Flores University Students

> Tags: `#story` `#contamination` `#university` `#students` `#youth`
>
> **Source:** El Grito Estudiantil
> **Authors:** Ana Rodriguez and Luis Garcia, Student Journalists

## Overview

A landmark student-written piece documenting how the Río de las Flores contamination crisis has seeped into every facet of life for students at Las Flores National University (LFNU), revealing anger, frustration, and fear about the future.

---

## Student Testimonies

### Maria — Environmental Science Major
> "I chose Las Flores National University because I wanted to study in a city known for its commitment to sustainability and innovation. But now, with our river literally dying, I'm starting to question if that was just a lie."

### Juan — Engineering Student & Protest Activist
> "We're supposed to be learning about cutting-edge technologies here, but all we can think about is how to save our city from total environmental collapse. It's like, what's the point of studying if there won't be a future for us here?"

### Sofia — Pre-Med Student
> "I've seen so many people getting sick, even my own cousins. I'm scared that by staying here to finish my degree, I'll end up making myself ill too."

## University Administration Response

The administration has been criticized for insufficient action:
- Some professors incorporate contamination discussions into classes
- Others seem reluctant to engage with the issue
- Students feel abandoned by institutional leadership

### Professor Hernandez Commentary
> "It's our duty as educators to confront this reality head-on and equip our students with the knowledge they need to understand and challenge what's happening in our city. The administration should be doing more to support us in that mission."

## Broader Impact

- Students questioning whether their futures lie in Las Flores
- Many feel they have no choice but to seek opportunities elsewhere
- The Río de las Flores disaster has claimed not just the river but the city's collective sense of hope and pride
