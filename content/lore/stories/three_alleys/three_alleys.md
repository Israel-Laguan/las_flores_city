## Narrative Purpose

These three parallel scenes serve several functions:

1. **Character establishment:** Each scene immediately defines the core trait of the character â€” Evelyn's immediate action, the student's resourceful survival, Alex's hesitant conscience.

2. **Moral spectrum:** Together they create a spectrum of response to crisis â€” from immediate action to reluctant participation to escape-and-pass. None is presented as purely right or wrong; each is appropriate to the character and their circumstances.

3. **Foreshadowing:** Each encounter prefigures the role the character will play in the larger narrative. Evelyn becomes the shadow operative. The student becomes the anonymous source. Alex becomes the reluctant leader who grows into the role.

4. **Thematic resonance:** The scenes echo each other to ask the central question of the narrative: *What does an ordinary person do when confronted with injustice?* The answer is different for everyone, and that's the point.

5. **City as character:** In all three scenes, the city of Las Flores itself is a presence â€” the apathetic crowds who walk past, the bystanders who film instead of help, the few who step forward. The city's moral condition is revealed in these moments of crisis.
