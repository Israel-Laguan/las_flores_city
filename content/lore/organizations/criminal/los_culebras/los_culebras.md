# Los Culebras

> Tags: `#organization` `#gang` `#crime` `#south_las_flores` `#minera_estrella` `#youth_recruitment`

**Aliases:** The Snakes, Culebras, Los Serpientes
**Type:** Street Gang / Criminal Organization
**Leader:** Unknown (hierarchical structure with rotating leaders)
**Territory:** South Las Flores, Old Las Flores, Vieja Las Flores
**Membership:** Primarily young men (14-25 years old) from working-class backgrounds

The **Los Culebras** gang operates in the South Las Flores district, specializing in low-level criminal activities that destabilize the community. Unlike larger organizations like the Flowers Syndicate, Los Culebras focuses on local control through intimidation, drug running, and recruitment of at-risk youth.

---

## Overview

Los Culebras ("The Snakes") emerged from the economic despair of South Las Flores, where traditional employment opportunities are scarce and corporate interests seek to acquire land cheaply. The gang preys on vulnerable youth, offering them a sense of purpose, protection, and quick money through criminal activities. Their name reflects both their stealthy operations and the venomous impact they have on the community.

The gang is notably **funded by Minera Estrella** as part of a corporate strategy to destabilize South Las Flores, making it easier for the company to acquire property at depressed prices for real estate development and mining expansion.

## Operations & Activities

### Primary Revenue Streams

1. **Package Running**
   - Delivery of drugs, weapons, and other contraband
   - Motorcycle couriers (like Alberto) transport packages across the district
   - Low-level but essential to the gang's operations

2. **Extortion & Protection**
   - Targets local businesses, community organizations, and residents
   - "Protection money" demanded under threat of vandalism or violence
   - Part of the destabilization strategy funded by Minera Estrella

3. **Drug Distribution**
   - Street-level sales of cocaine, marijuana, and synthetic drugs
   - Focuses on working-class neighborhoods
   - Uses youth recruits as sellers to avoid detection

4. **Vandalism & Intimidation**
   - Graffiti tagging to mark territory
   - Property damage to intimidate opponents
   - Community center attacks (as seen in beat_sofia_corruption_network)

### Territorial Control

- **South Las Flores:** Primary operational base
- **Old Las Flores (Vieja Las Flores):** Historic center with dense population
- **Río de las Flores riverbanks:** Used for hidden meetings and package drops
- **Agricultural Valley periphery:** Rural areas for hiding contraband

---

## Corporate Connection: Minera Estrella

Los Culebras receives **funding and direction** from Minera Estrella to:

1. **Destabilize South Las Flores** - Create an environment of fear and lawlessness
2. **Depress Property Values** - Make land acquisition cheaper for corporate development
3. **Disrupt Community Organization** - Prevent residents from uniting against corporate interests
4. **Recruit At-Risk Youth** - Remove potential opposition by giving youth criminal records

This relationship is **unknown to most gang members**, who believe they are operating independently. Only the leadership is aware of the corporate backing.

## Social Impact

### Community Destruction

Los Culebras contributes to the **erosion of community cohesion** in South Las Flores through:

- **Youth Recruitment:** Targets vulnerable teenagers with promises of money and status
- **Fear Campaigns:** Intimidates residents who speak out against gang activity
- **Property Damage:** Vandalizes community spaces and organizations
- **Drug Epidemic:** Floods neighborhoods with addictive substances

### Economic Exploitation

- **Exploits Desperation:** Recruits from families facing economic hardship
- **Creates Dependency:** Gang income becomes essential for many members' families
- **Blocks Development:** Discourages legitimate business investment in the district
