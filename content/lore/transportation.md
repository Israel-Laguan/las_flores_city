> Tags: `#transportation` `#infrastructure` `#metro` `#buses` `#streets` `#urban_design` `#Informational`

## Overview

Las Flores' meticulously planned streets, zones, and transportation networks illustrate a city designed for both functionality and beauty. The road and public transit systems ensure every part of the city is accessible, facilitating economic activity, cultural engagement, and leisure. The thoughtful layout not only supports the daily needs of residents but also encourages exploration and enjoyment of the city's diverse offerings.

To ensure that the city center remains navigable and not overcrowded, while still being well connected, Central Las Flores incorporates a network of streets, along with strategically placed bus and train stations.

## Design Considerations

### Pedestrian Zones
Areas around cultural landmarks, such as Teatro Nacional and La Casa de la MГєsica, are designated as pedestrian zones, encouraging foot traffic and reducing vehicular congestion in the city center.

### Bicycle Lanes
Along all major calles and avenues, dedicated bicycle lanes are implemented to promote cycling as a preferred mode of transportation for short distances, contributing to a reduction in central city crowding.

### Public Transportation Priority
Both bus and train stations are integrated with major destinations like the financial district and Universidad Nacional, ensuring that public transport is the most convenient option, thereby minimizing private vehicle use in the city center.

This ensures that Central Las Flores remains accessible and vibrant without compromising the tranquility and navigability of its core areas. The design prioritizes a balance between accessibility, functionality, and the preservation of the city's aesthetic and cultural charm.
