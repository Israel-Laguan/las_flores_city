# Río de las Flores

> Tags: `#landmark` `#river` `#natural` `#waterway`

## Overview

**Type:** River / Natural Landmark
**Location:** Flows through Andes, Agricultural Valley, Old Las Flores, Port Area, Pacific Delta
**Length:** Complete watershed from Andean headwaters to Pacific Ocean
**Key Features:** Mountain headwaters, fertile valley, urban waterway, coastal delta

The Río de las Flores (River of the Flowers) is the lifeblood of Las Flores Free City, originating high in the Andean Mountains and flowing westward through diverse landscapes to empty into the Pacific Ocean. Its journey shapes the geography, economy, and culture of all districts it touches.

---

## Geography & Course

### Mountain Headwaters
The river begins high in the Andean Mountains, where it is fed by melting snow and rainfall. This mountainous region is characterized by steep slopes and rugged terrain, providing a natural source of water that flows downwards.

### Agricultural Valley
As the river descends from the mountains, it carves a path through a rural valley dotted with small hills and home to indigenous communities. The river meanders through this valley, creating fertile land that supports agriculture.

### Old Las Flores
The river flows through Old Las Flores, where it plays a crucial role in the local ecosystem and community life. This area is characterized by a blend of rural and urban elements, with small farms and traditional homes situated along the riverbanks.

### Port Waterway
After passing through Old Las Flores, the river flows further west towards the Port Area. Here, it becomes an important waterway for trade and transportation, connecting the city to the Pacific Ocean.

### Pacific Delta
Ultimately, the Río de las Flores empties into the Pacific Ocean, creating a delta region that supports diverse wildlife and ecosystems. The river's mouth is a vital area for fishing and tourism, contributing to the local economy.
