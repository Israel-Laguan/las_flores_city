# La Casa de la Música

> Tags: `#landmark` `#music` `#entertainment` `#independent` `#central_city`

**Location:** Central City, Cultural Zone
**Type:** Independent live music venue, co-operatively owned
**Founded:** 2044 by Afro-Dutch and Latino musicians

## Overview

La Casa de la Música operates from a decommissioned textile warehouse in Central City, its loading bays converted to stage entrances. Acquired through a co-operative ownership model, it has remained structurally independent from LW Group's consolidation of the arts sector. The venue serves as both performance space and community gathering point, with its history marked by the 2058 industrial riots when it functioned as an informal mutual-aid coordination center.

By 2077 it operates as Central City's most consequential independent live music venue — commercially modest, politically inconvenient to certain interests, and impossible to acquire.

## Programming

The venue runs afternoon open-mic sessions from 14:00 to 18:30, featuring a deliberately permissive format spanning acoustic sets, spoken word, and experimental performances. Evening concerts regularly sell to capacity with programming ranging from Afro-Dutch percussive electronica to political cabaret.

The holographic light rig — a seventy-two-unit ceiling array installed in 2072 using municipal arts grant funding — transforms the stage into immersive light environments. LW Group lobbied to redirect that grant toward Teatro Nacional's renovation; the attempt failed by two votes.

## Artist Residency Program

A six-week residency program has run since 2047. The physical noticeboard near the main entrance lists current and upcoming residents, including alumni now represented by LW Group's cultural management subsidiary — a fact the board does not advertise.

## Community Archive

The climate-controlled Co-operative Records Archive stores physical recordings, set lists, and documentation from the venue's thirty-three year history. Accessible by appointment to researchers and residency alumni; includes partially redacted 2058 community coordination documents.
