# Neighborhoods near factories

> Tags: `#landmark` `#industrial` `#residential` `#working-class`

## Overview

When the Chinese mines first opened, workers seeking proximity to the mines and the city started occupying land in this area. Despite some workers settling in the valley, where services and roads were superior, the Industrial Zone district saw a significant population increase due to its strategic location. As time passed, the influx of immigrants and the expansion of both Chinese and Dutch mining operations catalyzed the transformation of the landscape from rural to industrial.

With the constitution of a new modern city to the north, a massive wave of immigrants further propelled the growth in this zone. Various factories sprouted up, leading to a diversification of the area's economy and a surge in housing demand.

---

## Notable Places

### Old Settlers' Nook
Some of the original houses of the early settlers can still be found scattered throughout the district, many abandoned yet standing as remnants of a bygone era.

### New Millennium Row
This area features modern buildings constructed between 2000 and 2050, showcasing various architectural styles and catering to different income levels.

### Factory-Adjacent Residences
Located near the factories, these residential areas are predominantly inhabited by the working class.

---

## Conclusion

The Industrial Zone District remains a testament to Las Flores' evolution, embodying the city's spirit of resilience and adaptability. Despite grappling with socio-economic disparities, environmental concerns, and safety issues, this sprawling district continues to thrive as the industrial heart of the city.
