# La Casa de la Música

> Tags: `#landmark` `#central-city` `#entertainment` `#music`

## Overview

La Casa de la Música was founded in 2044 by a coalition of Afro-Dutch and Latino musicians who had grown tired of watching the city's cultural calendar fall under the quiet gravitational pull of LW Group's commercial arts foundation. The building they occupied — a decommissioned textile warehouse in the Central City district, its loading bays converted to stage entrances — was acquired through a co-operative ownership model that has, so far, insulated it from acquisition.

The 2058 industrial riots marked the moment La Casa de la Música calcified into something more than a venue: in the weeks following the riots it served as an informal community gathering space, a rumour-exchange and mutual-aid coordination point, and briefly as an overflow medical station. That history saturates its walls.

## Notable Places

### Main Performance Floor
The converted textile warehouse's central space, capacity three hundred standing. Acoustic panels line the walls; the holographic light rig occupies the ceiling.

### Holographic Light Rig
A seventy-two-unit ceiling array of independently articulated projection modules, installed in 2072 using municipal arts grant funding that LW Group lobbied to redirect.

### Afternoon Open-Mic Stage
A smaller acoustic configuration of the main floor, set up daily from 14:00 for the open-mic sessions.

### Artist Residency Board
A physical noticeboard near the main entrance listing current residency holders and scheduled residents.

### Co-operative Records Archive
A climate-controlled back-room archive of physical recordings, set lists, and documentation from the venue's entire thirty-three year history.

---

## Conclusion

La Casa de la Música endures in 2077 as the rare Central City institution whose independence is structural rather than rhetorical. Its co-operative ownership model and its accumulated cultural capital have kept it outside the orbit of LW Group's consolidation of the arts sector.
