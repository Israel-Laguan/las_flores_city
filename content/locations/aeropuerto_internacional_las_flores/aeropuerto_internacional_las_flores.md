# Aeropuerto Internacional Las Flores

> Tags: `#landmark` `#central-city` `#transport` `#infrastructure`

## Overview

The Aeropuerto Internacional Las Flores began as a regional airfield serving the pre-constitutional provisional government in 2033 and was expanded in stages as the city formalised and its economy diversified. The decisive expansion came in 2047, when LW Group's logistics division secured a forty-year concession for dedicated cargo terminal infrastructure — a deal that brought the capital for the airport's third runway, its expanded international terminal, and the automated cargo-processing wing that today handles the majority of mineral export volume leaving Las Flores by air.

The concession structure effectively bifurcated the airport's identity from the outset: the public-facing international terminal handles commercial passenger traffic and tourism arrivals; the LW Group charter terminal in the airport's northern quadrant handles executive personnel, sensitive cargo, and the periodic international regulatory delegations whose visits to Las Flores are managed, in terms of schedule and access, by the company's government relations division.

---

## Notable Places

### Public International Terminal
The main passenger-facing terminal handling forty-two scheduled routes daily. Biometric gates log arrivals and departures against dual registries: city border authority and LW Group security division.

### LW Group Charter Terminal
A dedicated terminal in the airport's northern quadrant handling executive personnel, sensitive cargo, and regulatory delegations. Not accessible from the public concourse; operational twenty-four hours.

### Cargo Processing Wing
Autonomous cargo handling infrastructure in the southern freight complex, managing mineral export volume under the 2047 concession. Busiest operational period is overnight, timed for Asian market cargo windows.

### Biometric Gate Array
Passenger checkpoint infrastructure using facial and iris recognition across all public terminal access points. Data is maintained in dual registries.

## Conclusion

The Aeropuerto Internacional Las Flores exists in the public imagination as a gateway to the city — the first and last infrastructure most international visitors encounter. Its actual function in the city's economy is more precisely a gateway for the commodity that has defined Las Flores's development trajectory: lithium, in processed form, leaving the Andean range on schedules calibrated to exchange markets the arrivals board does not display.
