# Las Flores Free City

> Tags: `#city`

## Overview

Las Flores Free City stands as a sprawling metropolis of approximately 270,000 residents, spread across roughly 150 square kilometers between the Pacific coastline and the Andean Mountains. The city stretches about 20 kilometers from the ocean eastward to the mountain foothills, and approximately 10 kilometers north to south.

Its geography tells a story of contrasts: the Pacific Ocean defines its western boundary, while the Andean Mountains rise to the east. To the south, rivers cut through desert landscapes, and to the north, swamps transition into more Amazonian terrain.

---

## Notable Places

### Pacific Coastal District
The western urban edge where the city meets the ocean, featuring ports, beaches, and coastal businesses.

### Andean Mountain Corridor
The eastern boundary where urban development meets mountain foothills, offering both residential expansion and natural reserves.

### Old Las Flores
The historic southern heart of the city, preserving colonial architecture and cultural traditions near the river valley.

### Northern Elite Enclaves
Upscale neighborhoods in the north and northeast, home to modern infrastructure, luxury residences, and exclusive amenities.

---

## Conclusion

Las Flores Free City is a microcosm of geographic and social diversity, spanning from ocean to mountains and from desert to swamps. Its 270,000 inhabitants occupy a landscape where wealth and poverty, history and modernity, nature and urbanization exist in constant dialogue.
