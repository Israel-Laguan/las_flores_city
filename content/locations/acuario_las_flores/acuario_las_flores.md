# Acuario Las Flores

> Tags: `#landmark` `#port-area` `#entertainment`

## Overview

The Acuario Las Flores stands at the mouth of the Río de las Flores delta, a monument to the city's complicated relationship with its own waters. When the facility first opened in 2041 it was conceived as a conservation showcase. The 2052 Great Lithium Leak forced a fundamental reinvention, pivoting to an LW Group-sponsored corporate research partnership.

The contamination legacy remains visible in the permanent "Resilience Wing," where visitors can read the officially approved account of the Leak — one that conspicuously omits the suppressed environmental-liability report filed by the city's own marine biologists.

---

## Notable Places

### Holographic Coral Hall
The facility's centrepiece gallery, where floor-to-ceiling holographic projectors reconstruct reef formations destroyed by the 2052 Lithium Leak.

### LW Group Marine Research Wing
Corporate-sponsored genetics laboratory and bioreactor suite accessible on the standard visitor route.

### Resilience Wing
Permanent exhibition framing the post-2052 rebuilding effort as a story of institutional perseverance.

### Bioluminescent Jellyfish Gallery
Evening-hours installation featuring genetically modified jellyfish cultures in wall-length tanks.

### Private Event Terrace
Rooftop venue overlooking the Río de las Flores delta, available for corporate bookings.

---

## Conclusion

The Acuario Las Flores occupies an uncomfortable position in the city's cultural landscape: a public institution that would not exist in its current form without the corporate patronage of the entity most responsible for its original decline.
