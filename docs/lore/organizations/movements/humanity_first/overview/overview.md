# Humanity First

> Tags: `#humanity_first` `#global_movement` `#civil_society`

## Overview

**Type:** Global Movement
**Founded:** Response to environmental degradation, social inequality, and technological exploitation
**Las Flores Arrival:** March 2035 (Pioneer: Daniel Carter from Canada)
**Status in 2077:** Deeply embedded in Las Flores' culture and governance

Humanity First is a global movement centered on principles that prioritize human well-being, sustainability, and collective progress. In Las Flores, it has played a transformative role — shaping values, policies, and community identity over four decades.

## Core Principles (Summary)

1. Humanity Above Individuals
2. Sustainable Long-Term Survival
3. Diverse Strategies for Success
4. Merit-Based Recognition
5. Cooperative Collaboration
6. Support and Assistance
7. Historical, Knowledge, Environmental, and Cultural Stewardship
8. Equitable Access to Resources
9. Leaders' Accountability
10. Technology for Good

## Key Impact on Las Flores

### Sustainable Development
- Green infrastructure, renewable energy, waste reduction programs
- Environmental stewardship as city policy

### Social Justice
- Universal healthcare, free education, affordable housing initiatives
- Equitable access to resources

### Technological Innovation
- Hub for tech companies prioritizing social impact
- Technology for good as guiding principle

### Cultural Integration
- Two-way cultural exchange with Latin American traditions
- Adapted to local norms while introducing transparency and accountability
- Annual Cultural Festival (since 2049)

### Civic Engagement
- Community participation, volunteerism, community-led initiatives
- Fostered collective responsibility and social cohesion
