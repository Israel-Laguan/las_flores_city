## 2040: Accountability and Rogue Group Aftermath

### February — Internal Audit & Funding Freeze
- Council audits Diego & Carla's projects
- Evidence of misused funds and incomplete work found
- Council suspends funding publicly — signals transparency commitment

### July — Diego & Carla's Deflection Campaign
- Social media campaign portraying themselves as victims
- Paint council as "out of touch with real needs"
- Some sympathy, but majority supports council

### December — Final Exposure & Banishment
- Audit findings made public with photographic evidence (staged cleanups, misallocated funds)
- Community sentiment turns against them
- Diego & Carla formally expelled
- Council hosts community meeting; discusses prevention measures

## 2041: Structural Reforms

### March — New Accountability Measures
In wake of rogue group scandal:
- **Photographic/video documentation** required for all funded projects
- **Third-party witnesses** for progress verification
- **Regular progress updates** mandatory
- **Community Oversight Committee** established — respected locals verify project progress

### 2041 — Historical Preservation Committee Created

**Responsibilities:**
- **Documenting Key Events:** Major successes and challenges archived
- **Monthly Learning Sessions:** Revisiting key incidents with newer members
- **Annual Reports:** Summary of activities, challenges, and key lessons for transparency

**Impact:**
- **Shared Memory:** Preserved stories serve as cautionary tales; reinforce core values
- **Guiding Future Leaders:** Historical records invaluable during leadership transitions

## 2042–2049: Consolidation and Cultural Influence

### April 2042 — Worker-Owned Cooperative
- Inspired by HF's emphasis on collective well-being
- Provides affordable local services and job opportunities
- Backed by council; reduces reliance on traditional corporations

### July 2044 — Las Flores Community Fund
- Locals contribute small amounts for public projects (parks, clean-ups, education)
- Managed by council with Community Oversight Committee oversight

### 2045 — International Expansion of Historical Preservation

**Humanity First Archives (Global Digital Repository):**
- Centralized historical record accessible to all branches
- Includes project reports, audits, community feedback, notable incidents
- Each region contributes important documents
- **Historical Preservation Coordinators** appointed per region

**Biennial History Summit:**
- Coordinators present significant events and lessons from each region
- Cross-pollination of ideas and strategies
- Regions facing similar challenges learn from each other

### October 2045 — Youth Council Expansion
- Sofia Alvarez leads workshops on leadership, activism, and sustainable development
- New generation of young leaders inspired
- Youth Council collaborates with local schools; HF principles integrated into education

### 2045–2046 — **Valentina Rojas Incident** *(see [Rogue Incidents](rogue_incidents.md))*

### May 2047 — Environmental Resilience Project
- Multi-year project: tree planting, river cleanup, stronger environmental regulation advocacy
- Attracts international attention and support

### December 2049 — Humanity First Cultural Festival
- Celebrates a decade of HF presence in Las Flores
- Showcases local art, music, and community projects
- Thousands of attendees
- **Becomes an annual tradition** — reinforces cultural integration
