# La Coalición por la Justicia Social (CJS)

> Tags: `#civil_society` `#indigenous` `#environmental` `#grassroots`

## Overview

**Type:** Grassroots Advocacy Organization
**Leader:** Ana Ramirez
**Mission:** Indigenous community rights and environmental protection
**Origin:** Response to a law threatening to privatize riverbank indigenous communities

---

## Origin Story

CJS was born from resistance. When a controversial law aimed to privatize sections of the riverbank — where several indigenous communities resided — these communities banded together to fight for their right to remain on ancestral lands.

### First Victory
- Successfully negotiated a settlement allowing some indigenous communities to retain portions of their riverbank land
- Required to delimit territory and relinquish claims to privatized areas
- A significant triumph for the fledgling organization

---

## Activities

- Protesting against mining expansions and deforestation
- Advocating for laws benefiting marginalized groups
- Active presence in rural and riverbank areas
- Engagement with far south desert communities
- Direct community outreach to identify pressing issues
