# Luz del Rio

## Overview
Luz del Rio was a significant infrastructure company founded in the mid-2030s that became one of the original Public-Private Partnership (PPP) partners in the **Las Flores Dam Authority** in 2035, alongside Great Dragon Energy and EBF. It provided the electrical grid infrastructure critical to powering the Free City's early growth. By 2057, after losing contracts and being scapegoated in the Minera Estrella trial, it had been reduced to a small electric company and ultimately driven to bankruptcy.

---

## Original Role: Dam Authority PPP Partner (2035)

Luz del Rio was founded as a major infrastructure company and became one of the three original partners in the **Las Flores Dam Authority** in 2035, alongside Great Dragon Energy and EBF (European Energy & Infrastructure). Its primary function was providing the electrical grid infrastructure that powered the Free City's rapid expansion — managing hydroelectric distribution from the San Miguel Dam and maintaining high-voltage transmission lines across all 11 districts.

As a PPP partner, Luz del Rio held significant influence over city infrastructure planning. It contracted with Minera Estrella to power mining operations, processing facilities, and port infrastructure, making it deeply entangled with the Chinese conglomerate's business interests. During this period, the company employed several hundred people and held long-term service contracts with the city government.

---

## Decline Phase (1950s–2057)

After the Great Lithium Leak of 2052, the political winds shifted. The LW Group and Minera Estrella needed a scapegoat to absorb blame and financial liability. Luz del Rio — by then a smaller, politically weaker player compared to the Chinese giants — became the ideal target.

By the time of the trial, Luz del Rio had already been reduced from a major infrastructure partner to a small electric company servicing only a handful of industrial clients. The company had lost key public contracts amid the political turmoil following the disaster, as larger Chinese and European energy interests consolidated control of city infrastructure.

---

## The Scapegoating & Trial (2057)

In March 2057, Minera Estrella sued Luz del Rio, claiming a faulty electrical component caused the leak. This was part of the cover-up narrative that blamed an electrical failure for the disaster. Despite Evelyn Ruthenberg's attempts to present evidence of the real cover-up, the evidence was not used in the trial. The defense team received threats, and the trial was rigged from the beginning.

---

## Bankruptcy (December 2057)

Luz del Rio filed for bankruptcy in December 2057 after losing critical contracts and paying damages from the rigged trial. The company lost its assets and ceased operations. **Evelyn Ruthenberg**, who had worked as an engineer at Luz del Rio before the disaster, became one of the company's most prominent voices in exposing the injustice — and later led the restoration efforts that sought to undo the damage caused by the very leak Luz del Rio had been blamed for.
