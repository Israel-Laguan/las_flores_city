# Karla's Murder (Evelyn's Encounter)

>
> **Date:** February 2053
> **Key Characters:** Evelyn Ruthenberg, Karla (Victim), Nubia (Sister, later found dead)
> **Locations:** Las Flores City Streets, A Secluded Alleyway, Karla & Nubia's Apartment

---

## Overview

"Karla's Murder" serves as the visceral prologue to Evelyn Ruthenberg's journey — the moment a young woman preoccupied with trivialities is violently thrust into the deadly corporate conspiracy surrounding Las Flores' lithium exploitation. This is the first of three parallel "ordinary person faces crisis" scenes that bookend the narrative, deliberately echoing each other to examine how different people respond when confronted with the opportunity to help.

---

## The Incident

On an otherwise beautiful, sunlit afternoon in Las Flores, **Evelyn Ruthenberg** — then a young engineer at Luz del Río — is walking through the bustling streets, distracted by her phone while debating whether to buy a jacket online. She thinks about telling her friend Sarah about the jacket, imagines how Sarah would "totally rock it too." Math is not her forte, but finding deals is her superpower. The jacket dilemma feels crucial, the struggle real.

The air buzzes with chatter and car horns. The smell of street food makes her stomach rumble. The sun beats down, warming her skin as she navigates the busy streets. Las Flores is looking especially beautiful today — the mountains on one side, the ocean on the other.

Then everything changes.

A bang. A scream. The sharp sound echoes off the buildings, piercing the air and sending a shiver down her spine.

For a moment, everything stops. Some people look worried briefly, then hurry on. A few girls whisper to each other, looking scared, but no one does anything. The city holds its breath for a moment, then exhales, moving on. Everyone just keeps walking — as if nothing happened.

But Evelyn freezes, stuck between her small worries and this big, terrifying thing that just happened. *"What if something really bad happened? What if someone needs help? Oh gosh, what if they're hurt?"*

She knows she should just keep walking, like everyone else. But something inside her compels her forward.

---

## The Dying Whistleblower

In the grimy alley, Evelyn finds a young woman — **Karla**, approximately 24 years old — huddled against the grimy brick wall. Her once-vibrant yellow dress is stained crimson. Her dark hair, styled in neat braids, is matted with blood and sweat, framing a face etched with pain yet somehow still radiating defiance. The metallic scent of blood fills the air, thick and cloying.

Karla has been shot and severely slashed with a long knife, her right arm severed in several places.

She mistakes Evelyn for a man — "Sir, please call an ambulance" — perhaps because of the dark alley, or perhaps because she thinks a woman wouldn't venture into a crime scene. When she sees Evelyn clearly, there's a moment of recognition.

Overwhelmed by the brutal reality and possessing no first-aid training, Evelyn tries to comfort Karla but inadvertently causes her pain while trying to remove her left shoe as Karla requests. Even then, Karla endures it — a testament to her strength — and does not scold Evelyn.

Karla begins crying. Evelyn cries with her.

---

## The Handoff

Despite her agony, Karla has a singular mission. She forces Evelyn to take three items hidden in her left shoe:
1. **A Key**
2. **A Small Green Pouch** (containing money)
3. **A Note** for Nubia

Karla's instructions are explicit: tell Nubia to keep the money and **run from the city**.

But Evelyn forgets to ask who Nubia is or how to find her. She assumes Karla will tell her more.

Karla grows drowsy. "I'm relieved you came," she says, starting to close her eyes. Evelyn, not wanting to bother her, lets her rest. The sirens are getting closer, so she focuses on the paramedics. She doesn't realize those are Karla's last words — thanking her for being there so she wouldn't die alone.

The guilt will gnaw at Evelyn for years. She should have held her hand. Should have reassured her, told her that Nubia would be okay.

---

## The Aftermath

The paramedics arrive too late. Evelyn is left traumatized, her hands covered in blood, her worldview permanently altered. The jacket dilemma and the trivialities of daily life are replaced by a desperate, crushing guilt and a newfound resolve.

She did not know it then, but this encounter set off a chain reaction. The items Karla gave her were the key to unlocking the deepest secrets of the Minera Estrella cover-up.

---

## The Apartment Discovery (September 2053)

Months later, Evelyn gathers the courage to investigate. She returns to the neighborhood and asks around. Someone remembers Karla's face — a middle-aged man who couldn't recall anything else. That gives Evelyn the courage to try entering the apartment.

The door is easy to open, just using the knob. She wears medical gloves. No one is inside; she opens all the rooms — the bathroom, the closet. It's a small apartment. It's near the end of the month, so the landlord hasn't been poking around.

Inside, she finds:
- **Photos and bills** with Karla and Nubia's names — they share the same surnames (sisters or cousins)
- **Food in the fridge**, the bed made, even the trash in the bathroom still there — Karla "just vanished"
- Signs one of them was **vegetarian**
- A **small wooden box** that the killers didn't notice — unlocked by the key Karla gave her

Inside the box:
- A copy of **Cecilia Pérez's suppressed study** showing lithium resistance a year before the public disaster
- **Internal emails** between Minera Estrella executives discussing structural issues before the leak
- A handful of **Carinata seeds** from the green pouch — plants that surprisingly resisted the contamination
- **Bills and documents** showing they had lived in the apartment for a long time

Evelyn leaves with the evidence, understanding now that the green pouch contained Carinata seeds Karla wanted studied. Karla had been a **local farmer** trying to demand the mining company compensate for contaminating her Carinata oil plantation with lithium hydroxide — the waste had escaped from a pipe explosion at the mining facility, reached a river affluent, and contaminated her crop.

From this point, Evelyn carries the evidence, lost as to what to do — it felt bigger than her. She continued her life, working at Luz del Río, but the seed of justice had been planted.

## Thematic Resonance

Karlas's murder is the first of three parallel "ordinary person faces crisis" scenes in the Las Flores narrative:

| Encounter | Person | Victim | Response |
|-----------|--------|--------|----------|
| **Evelyn & Karla** (Feb 2053) | Young Luz del Río engineer | Dying whistleblower (female, shot) | Rushes in, helps, receives evidence, is haunted |
| **The Student & The Whistleblower** (Aug 2059) | University student | Stabbed office worker (female) | Receives USB, escapes assassin, eventually passes evidence to Evelyn |
| **Alex & The Car Crash** (early 2077) | Architecture student | Accident victims (couple) | Initially freezes, helps after others act, is haunted by his hesitation |

Each scene examines a different response to crisis — Evelyn acts immediately, the student acts under pressure and escapes, Alex hesitates and then follows others' lead. Together they form a triptych of ordinary courage.
