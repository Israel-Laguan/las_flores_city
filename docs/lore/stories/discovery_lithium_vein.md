# Discovery of the Lithium Vein

> Tags: `#dutch_interest` `#lithium` `#science`
> 
> **Location:** Near Río de las Flores, Old Las Flores
> **Period:** 2029

## Overview
In 2029, Global Lithium Corp (GLC) sent a small group of scientists and engineers to investigate rumors of a significant lithium deposit near Las Flores. Led by the renowned geologist Dr. Annabella van der Meer, the team was determined to uncover the truth behind the whispers of untapped mineral wealth.

## The Discovery
Annabella and her team arrived in Las Flores, eager to begin their survey. They were guided by a local man named Miguel, who had grown up in the area and knew the terrain like the back of his hand. Miguel led them to an old, abandoned mine near the river, a place where locals had once mined for small amounts of minerals.

As they ventured deeper into the mine, they approached an underground river, and Annabella wanted to drink the water. Miguel warned her not to drink the water. "The water here is poisoned," he said, his voice echoing through the dark tunnels. "It has made many people sick over the years."

Annabella, always the curious scientist, had a sudden inspiration. "If the water is making people sick, it could be due to high concentrations of lithium," she mused in Dutch. She quickly took a sample of the water, sealing it in a sterile container for later analysis.

Back in their makeshift lab in Old Las Flores, Annabella ran tests on the water sample. Her suspicions were confirmed: the water contained unusually high levels of lithium, a clear indication that there was a substantial vein nearby. Excited by her discovery, she shared the news with Pieter, her husband, who was equally thrilled.

## The Team's Illness
Annabella assembled a new team to confirm the presence of the lithium vein. However, in her enthusiasm, she neglected to share her findings about the poisonous water with the new group. The team, unaware of the danger, entered the mine without the necessary precautions.

As they worked, several members of the team began to feel ill. They experienced headaches, nausea, and dizziness — all symptoms of lithium poisoning. Despite their discomfort, they managed to collect the necessary samples and make their way back to the surface.

Back in Old Las Flores, the team members were rushed to the hospital. Annabella, realizing her mistake, felt a wave of guilt. She quickly arranged to cover all their medical expenses and ensured they received the best possible care.

To her surprise, the team members did not blame her. Instead, they thanked her for her support and handed over the samples they had collected. The samples confirmed the presence of a massive lithium vein, far larger than anyone had anticipated.

## Impact
Annabella and Pieter were deeply moved by the resilience and forgiveness of the local team members. They admired the strength and spirit of the Latin American locals and vowed to always support and help the community in any way they could. This experience shaped the Van der Meer family's philosophy, instilling in them a deep respect for the local population and a commitment to their well-being.
