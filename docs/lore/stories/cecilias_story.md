## Phase 1: Safety Auditor (~2047â€“2048)

Before her research career, Cecilia had been hired by Minera Estrella as a **safety auditor**. Her biology background made her well-suited to evaluate chemical containment protocols and environmental safety procedures. During her audits, she documented structural weaknesses in the facility's container systems and prepared detailed reports flagging leakage risks.

Her findings were **suppressed** by LW Group leadership â€” her reports were locked away rather than acted upon. This suppression became the catalyst for her next phase: rather than abandon her work through official channels, she sought an independent path to prove what she had observed.

## Phase 2: The Grant and Independent Research (2049)

Cecilia obtained a **university research grant** with a carefully crafted proposal: *"a comparative study of native and introduced plant species and their influence on the local ecosystem."* She deliberately avoided any mention of lithium contamination or the cover-up â€” she knew referencing the scandal would doom the application. Half genuine scientific curiosity, half calculated deception, the grant gave her institutional cover and access to Minera Estrella property to set up experimental fields.

Her true objective was to investigate a puzzle she had first noticed during her audits: certain plants thriving in areas that should have been heavily contaminated by lithium runoff.

## Phase 3: The Research
Once her grant was approved, Cecilia set up an experimental field on a less-monitored part of Minera Estrella's property. She noticed certain plants thriving unexpectedly in the polluted areas surrounding the containment sites.

One plant, in particular, caught Cecilia's attention due to its remarkable resilience and apparent ability to flourish in the lithium-contaminated soil. Recognizing the significance of this discovery, she saw an opportunity that could redefine her career and contribute to the field of phytoremediation.

## The Research
Even as news of the "Great Leak" began to emerge, Cecilia pressed on with her research, setting up a simple experimental field on a less monitored part of Minera Estrella's property. Her work involved not just the resilient plant discovered near the leakage but also required her to source specific species for comparison, including a trip to San Pedro de Los Pescadores. Despite the challenges, Cecilia managed to cultivate a thriving experimental field, meticulously recording the growth and health of the plants.

Cecilia's experiments yielded promising early results, indicating that her initial observations about the plant's resilience to lithium contamination were correct. With just about a month left to finalize her data collection and analyze her findings, she was on the brink of a significant breakthrough in phytoremediation research.

## Death
Tragically, Cecilia's life was cut short before she could complete her research. Her body was found in an alley in the City District, bearing multiple stab wounds. The official statement declared her death the result of a common thief, a random act of violence unrelated to her work.

Despite the official account, those close to Cecilia, including her roommate Nubia, could not shake the suspicion that her death was connected to her groundbreaking research. The timing of her demise â€” just as she was nearing the conclusion of her research and preparing to publish her findings â€” seemed too coincidental to ignore.

## The Cover-Up
Cecilia's dedication to her research and her significant discovery posed a threat to the interests of Minera Estrella and the LW Group. Her untimely death under suspicious circumstances highlights the potential dangers faced by individuals who uncover inconvenient truths for powerful entities. While the authorities dismissed her death as the result of a common crime, the timing and nature of her demise point to a more sinister possibility.

In her research from 2049, Cecilia discovered that the containment had been leaking long before the so-called "Great Leakage." Her evidence, including data from as early as 2050, threatened to expose the negligence of the LW Group and Minera Estrella. Aware of the potential legal and financial repercussions, Li Wei attempted to suppress her findings, first through bribery and then by applying pressure for her to abandon her investigation.

Despite the threats, Cecilia was determined to publish her findings. As she finalized her paper on Phytoremediation and prepared to unveil her discovery of the new species and the evidence of the prolonged leakage, Li Wei, in collaboration with Zhang Liang, made the ruthless decision to eliminate the threat she posed. Her murder was orchestrated to look like a random street robbery, leaving her research and the truth of her findings in limbo.
