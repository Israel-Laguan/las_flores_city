# Xue's Late Epiphany

> Tags: `#chinese_community` `#love` `#restaurant` `#identity`
> 
> **Location:** Old Las Flores, Xue's restaurant
> **Period:** ~2025–2059

## Overview
In the intricate web of Las Flores, there lived a peculiar woman, Xue, whose presence in the city was cloaked in mystery. Dispatched from China as part of a covert operation to keep tabs on influential figures overseeing the mines, she was the embodiment of dedication. She sent diligent reports, her loyalty unwavering despite the fact that the call to return home never came. Over time, it dawned on her that perhaps her assignment was less about her skills and more about her superiors finding a convenient way to cast her aside.

## From Soldier to Cook
Xue's cover was an unassuming small restaurant on the evolving streets of Las Flores. As the years passed, she morphed from a soldier to a cook, her trained hands now adept at wielding a spatula instead of a weapon. The city changed around her; modernization laid its claim, and her once-quaint eatery began to show its age amidst the gleaming new facades.

Among her sparse regulars was an indigenous man, aged around 50, who found solace in the familiar walls of her restaurant. He was drawn to her stern demeanor and the melancholy that hid beneath her stern facade. He flirted with her in his own reserved way, offering his help in her daily chores. Xue, 45 and acutely aware that her reports had become little more than a formality, found herself intrigued by his gentle persistence.

Xue found herself enjoying his presence, despite herself. She gave him chores, watched him work with the quiet intensity of an obedient creature. Sometimes she slipped extra money into his pay when he wasn't looking. It was their unspoken pact. To Xue, it was a relationship that defied labels — he was her "pet," a term far from derogatory in her mind; it signified a bond that they'd carved out from their unique circumstances.

## Whispers of Change
As years furthered and the city roared into a new era, the odd pair became a fixture in their own right. Whispers would follow them, speculations of a romantic entanglement, but she'd only laugh them off. Her indifference was a testament to her strength, her resolve not to let the judgments of others dictate her life.

It was in the year 2059, at the age of 59, when an official notice finally arrived, relieving her of her long-forgotten military duty. By then, her restaurant had become a relic, as much a part of her as the companionship she'd cultivated with the man who had never left her side.

The sun hung low, a smoldering ember in the Las Flores sky. Xue moved through her kitchen, a soldier's efficiency in every step. The city had changed around her — new buildings sprouted like steel flowers, but she remained steadfast. Kusi came daily, this man she'd taken to calling Kusi — a nickname that suited him better than his given name. He reminded her of a stray dog, always appearing at her door like clockwork.

As Xue scrubbed the kitchen floor with harsh strokes of a brush, Kusi appeared in the doorway with wood for dinner's fire. Their eyes met briefly before he set about building up the flames.

"You're late," Xue said without turning around.

Kusi nodded, "The market was busy."

Watching him there in the gathering dusk, Xue felt something shift inside her chest — something she couldn't quite name but that made her heart beat faster nonetheless.

"Yeah," she thought to herself quietly as shadows fell over the room and Kusi turned towards her expectantly.

"Yeah I would like to marry…"

Kusi stared at her for a moment before breaking into a wide grin. He stepped forward and took her hand in his calloused one, squeezing gently.

"I thought you'd never ask," he chuckled softly.
