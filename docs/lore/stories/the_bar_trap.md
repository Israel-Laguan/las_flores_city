## Overview

The Bar Trap is the pivotal moment when Alex Garcia and Evelyn Ruthenberg first met — not as hunter and hunted, but as two people who had been fighting the same war from opposite sides of the dark. It began with Alex's desperate gambit to force an encounter with the elusive vigilante, and ended with an unlikely alliance that would change the course of Las Flores.

---

## The Setup

Alex had discovered that one of Evelyn's remaining targets, a former town servant named Manuel, had a predictable routine. Every Tuesday, Manuel visited a local bar near the port, where he would drink alone, nursing his guilt and his whiskey. Alex saw this as an opportunity to create a trap — to force an encounter with the elusive Evelyn.

The plan was simple: tip Evelyn off anonymously that Manuel was vulnerable and an easy target. When Evelyn arrived, Alex would be waiting.

They knew it was dangerous. Evelyn had killed eight people over two decades. She was methodical, patient, and utterly without sentiment when it came to her targets. But Alex also knew that their mother's death was connected to the same people Evelyn was hunting. They needed answers. They needed the truth.

---

## The Encounter

Evelyn arrived at the bar as predicted, her eyes scanning the dimly lit room until they landed on Manuel. She was older than Alex had imagined — in her late forties, with the kind of tiredness that came from years of solitary purpose. She moved with the precision of someone who had learned to be invisible.

Before she could make her move, Alex revealed themselves, stepping out from a corner table.

Evelyn startled. Her hand went to her belt where a weapon was concealed. For a long moment, neither of them spoke.

Alex broke the silence quickly, telling Evelyn everything: about Isabela's kidnapping by the company's henchmen, about the urgent need for help, about the investigation at Residencial Las Estrellas and Carlos's death. The words came out in a rush, unpolished and desperate.

Evelyn listened. She did not interrupt.

When Alex finished, Evelyn studied them with an expression that was impossible to read. She had investigated Alex, she said. She knew they were not part of the mining company's influence. His passion for justice reminded her of her own comrades she had lost along the way.

She proposed a deal: she would help him rescue Isabela if he promised to join forces with her.

---

## The Revelation

Then Evelyn said something that changed everything.

She mentioned Alex's mother — Rosa García. She told Alex that the respiratory problems that had killed Rosa were caused by the lithium-contaminated groundwater, the direct result of Minera Estrella's negligence.

Alex was taken aback. They had never connected their mother's death to the water poisoning. There had been no official acknowledgment, no public record linking the coastal town's health crisis to the mining company. Evelyn's words stirred a newfound anger within them — a cold, specific fury that organized the grief they had carried for years.

Alex agreed to Evelyn's terms. Together, they began to plan the rescue mission.

---

## The Lake House Rescue

Evelyn's investigation led them to a secluded house near a small lake, where Isabela was being held. The property was remote, guarded by a small team of enforcers loyal to the mining company.

With careful precision, they infiltrated the property. Evelyn moved through the shadows, disabling guards with the same methodical efficiency she had applied to her HVAC sabotage. Alex followed, focused on reaching Isabela.

After a tense standoff, they managed to rescue Isabela. She was shaken but alive — her fiery spirit intact despite the days of captivity.

As Isabela and Alex reunited, Evelyn disappeared into the shadows. She reappeared later with a single piece of news: Manuel had been dealt with.

---

## The Alliance Forms

In the aftermath of the rescue, Evelyn contacted Alex, reminding them of their promise. Isabela, fueled by her own anger and desire for justice, decided to join them. Together, they formed an unlikely alliance.

To Alex's surprise, Evelyn was not the cold avenger they had imagined. She was a guardian, a protector who had suffered her own losses to the city's greed. She carried the weight of Yara Rossi's death, of the friends she had failed to protect, of the decades she had spent in the dark.

Alex brought something Evelyn had lost: a reason to believe the fight could be won without becoming a monster. They brought warmth, connection, and the ability to make people feel like resistance was survivable.

They negotiated over several weeks. The negotiations were not warm. But they built something that would become the foundation of the 2077 movement: Evelyn's tactical precision, Alex's human warmth, and the shared understanding that the city could not survive without both.
