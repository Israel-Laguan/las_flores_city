# The Forgotten Neighborhoods: Old Las Flores Struggles Against Rising Gang Violence

> Tags: `#story` `#old_las_flores` `#gang_violence` `#poverty` `#inequality`
>
> **Author:** Ana Rodriguez, Old Las Flores Correspondent (El Informador)

## Overview

An investigative piece documenting how Old Las Flores — a historic neighborhood once thriving as a commercial center — has been left behind amid the lithium boom, now struggling with high unemployment, decaying infrastructure, and an alarming surge in gang-related violence.

---

## The Disparity

While gleaming skyscrapers and trendy cafes in downtown draw international attention, Old Las Flores finds itself increasingly marginalized:
- High unemployment
- Decaying infrastructure
- Rising gang violence

### Crime Statistics
Violent crimes in Old Las Flores have surged by over **150%** in the past three years.

## Residents' Plight

> "The police barely patrol this part of town. It's like they think we're not worth protecting." — **Maria Hernandez**, longtime resident and community organizer

### Contrast with Wealthy Districts
- Upscale neighborhoods: frequent police patrols, rapid response times
- Old Las Flores: left to fend itself against increasingly brazen criminal element

## Criminal Exploitation

Local gang members have exploited the neighborhood's marginalized status:
- Extorting small business owners and residents
- Violence or intimidation against those who refuse to pay
- Undermining the already fragile economic foundation

## Community Response

Leaders like Maria Hernandez have organized:
- Protests and advocacy campaigns
- Demands for better policing, improved infrastructure, and targeted economic development

So far, pleas have gone largely unheeded by the city's leadership, who remain focused on courting foreign investors and elite residents.
