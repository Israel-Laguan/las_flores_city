The atmosphere was charged with emotion, but also tinged with an undercurrent of excitement. Las Flores had grown exponentially in recent years, transforming from a sleepy mining town into a thriving port city. The discovery of lithium deposits had drawn international attention and investment, with companies like China Lithium Corp and Van der Meer Mining striking deals to extract the valuable mineral.
The student wove through the crowd, dodging tables laden with pamphlets and petitions. As they neared the bus terminal, a sharp cry pierced the air. It came from a narrow alleyway branching off from the main corridor. The student hesitated, then turned towards the sound, their curiosity piqued.

They were a mix of ages and backgrounds - young students in uniform, middle-aged office workers, and elderly residents in traditional Andean dress. All seemed reluctant to approach, as if sensing the danger that still lingered.

Another man, his eyes wide with fear, was whispering into a walkie-talkie, likely alerting more security personnel or even the local authorities.
The atmosphere was heavy with unspoken questions and dread.
