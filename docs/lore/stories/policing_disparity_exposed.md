## Overview

A powerful critique exposing the glaring disparity in police presence between wealthy and impoverished neighborhoods in Las Flores, arguing that wealth — not need — dictates who gets protected.

---

## The Tale of Two Cities

- **Villa Dorada:** Security cameras on every corner, armed guards in gated communities
- **Barrio Viejo:** Crumbling buildings stand unguarded; desperate residents take matters into their own hands

## Root Causes

### The Business Coalition's Influence
Las Flores' Business Coalition — a powerful lobby representing wealthy investors, mining executives, and elites — has significant sway over city governance. They advocate for increased security in affluent neighborhoods, arguing it protects their investments and promotes "stability."

However, this focus comes at the expense of poorer communities. The Business Coalition has consistently opposed proposals to boost police presence or improve infrastructure in low-income districts.

### Political Power Imbalance
- Poor neighborhoods lack strong political representation
- Wealthy communities have organized advocacy groups and influential patrons
- The city council: only **Councilwoman Ana Silva** openly champions the underprivileged; the remaining six are either beholden to the Business Coalition or too timid to challenge it

> "Wealth should not dictate who is protected and who is left vulnerable." — **Juan Pablo Hernandez**

## Call to Action

Las Flores must address the inequity in policing and political representation. The essay demands fairness and equality for all residents.
