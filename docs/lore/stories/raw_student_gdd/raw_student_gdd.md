# Prologue: The Seed and The Trial (2053)

*(Note: The following events occur in February 2053, long before the 2077 rebellion. The narrator is a young **Evelyn Ruthenberg** when she was just starting her career as an engineer at Luz del Río. This tragic encounter with Karla is the catalyst for her entire awakening against Li Wei and the LW Group.)*

Cute jacket online, but is it too much? That was the biggest decision on my mind just moments ago. It's perfect for hanging out or maybe even for my next exam. Walking through Las Flores, I can't wait to tell Sarah about this jacket. She'd totally rock it too! Flipping through jacket options and comparing prices, trying to justify the purchase. Math's not my forte, but finding deals? That's my superpower. Still, the right jacket felt crucial, and the struggle was real. Annoyed, I finally looked up, taking in the city around me.

The air buzzed with the sounds of chatter and car horns, the smell of street food making my stomach rumble, everyone busy doing their own thing.  The sun beat down, warming my skin as I navigated the bustling streets. Las Flores is looking extra beautiful today, with the mountains on one side and the ocean on the other. It's crazy how different it is from the center, with all the tall buildings and crowded houses. In this lovely place, My jacket dilemma seem silly. But then everything changes - a bang. A scream. The sharp sound echoed off the buildings, piercing the air and sending a shiver down my spine.

For a moment, everything stops. Some people look worried for a bit, but then they just hurry on. A few girls whisper to each other, looking scared, but no one does anything. The city held its breath for a moment, then exhaled, moving on. Everyone just... kept walking? Like nothing happened? But I was frozen, stuck between my small worries and this big, scary thing that just happened. "What if something really bad happened? What if someone needs help? Oh gosh, what if they're hurt?" I know I should just keep walking, like everyone else. But something inside me... I just had to know. Curiosity killed the cat, right?

So there I am, stepping into something really scary, peeking into the alley. I chose to do this. No one forced me to. I'm the one who's usually just worried about school and fun stuff. And now, I'm right in the middle of something very real and scary. I'm the one who chokes at the sight of blood, but also the one who rushes to the door to find a girl drenched in red. The one who cried a lot later.

For a second, I feel totally lost. I remember looking at my hands, covered in her blood, and thinking, "This is human blood, from this girl. This is like something out of a nightmare... this can't be real." Why did I cry when she was dying? Was it the contrast between the beautiful day and this gruesome scene? Or something more? Maybe I'm too sentimental. "Please, help me," she barely says, and I feel so thrown off. My trivial concerns collided with the stark reality of life and death, and in that moment, something inside me shifted. The jacket, the coffee, all of it faded away, replaced by a desperate urge to act, to help, to change.

So, there's this girl, covered in blood and crying, I asked her name, Karla, while she huddled against the grimy brick wall of the alley, her once vibrant yellow dress stained crimson. Her dark hair, styled in neat braids, was matted with blood and sweat, framing a face etched with pain, yet somehow still radiating defiance. The metallic scent of blood filled the air, thick and cloying.

She's whispering, "Help, help me," in a tiny voice. She's crying, and when I approach, she looks visibly relieved. "Thank God! Please help me, I've been shot," she says. I wonder why she states the obvious. "Sir, please call an ambulance," she pleads, mistaking me for a man. Maybe it's the dark alley, or maybe she thinks a woman wouldn't venture into a crime scene. But when she sees me, I know I have to help.

She doesn't believe me when I tell her the ambulance will be here soon. She just keeps crying. Out of nowhere, she asks me to help her remove her left shoe. The poor girl, around 24 years old, is so frail and has no strength to move her right arm, which is severed in several places, maybe by a long knife. She tries to remove the shoe herself but winces in pain, so she asks me for help. She whispers for help, and suddenly, I'm not thinking about my small problems anymore. It's just me and her, and I'm ready to do whatever I can, even with zero experience.

Maybe that's when I start crying with her. I've never been in a situation like this before, never seen so much blood or pain. My parents and aunt had shielded me from hardship. I didn't even know basic first aid. And so, I hurt her when I try to remove her shoe. Even then, she endures it, a testament to her strength, and doesn't even scold me.

Karla tells me to take the key, a small green pouch, and a note to some Nubia, like I know who that is. She tells me to instruct Nubia to keep the money and run from the city. I promise to deliver it, no matter what. But I forget to ask who Nubia is or how to find her. Maybe I think Karla will tell me more later. This is so scary, but I can't just leave her, right?

Karla starts to grow drowsy. "I'm relieved you came," she says, starting to close her eyes. I don't want to bother her, so I let her rest. The sirens are getting closer, so I focus on the paramedics. I don't realize those are her last words, that she's thanking me for being with her. I'm just so ignorant. I should've held her hand, reassured her, told her that Nubia would be okay. The guilt gnaws at me, a heavy weight in my stomach. I'm just so ignorant.

# Chapter 1: 2077

*(Years later. The city has changed. Alex Garcia enters the scene.)*

"Crap! Crap! Double Crap!" Alex weaved through the crowded street market, the aroma of fried plantains and synthetic meat doing nothing to ease his anxiety. Professor Mendoza's class was starting in five, and his internal clock screamed like a siren. No empty chairs today, he just knew it. Missing another class meant losing the grant, and losing the grant... well, let's just say his dad's disappointed stare could melt steel.

Still, weaving through the crowds, Alex couldn't help but geek out at the cityscape. Alex, an aspiring architect, found solace in the city's design. Buildings that scraped the sky, sustainable technology woven into everyday life, a testament to human ingenuity. Solar lamps, self-driving taxis, buildings that looked like they were designed by aliens – this place was an architectural playground!

Every detail, from the bioluminescent trees that lit the paths at night to the kinetic pavements generating energy with each footstep, whispered of the future he yearned to build. This city, the  most advanced along South America's Pacific coast, trailing just behind  Santiago de Chile in its architectural and technological achievements, represented the future he dreamed of shaping. Shame he might get kicked out of the program before he could build his own futuristic masterpiece if he keeps being late to classes.

Lost in his admiration for the city's ingenuity, Alex was jolted back to reality by  the sudden sound of a crash nearby, pulling him out of his musings.
"Great," he thought, "just another reason to be late."

Through the shattered windshield of the sedan, Alex saw him – the driver, his face a mask of blood and terror, struggling to free himself from the crushed metal. But it was the woman beside him, slumped against the dashboard, her chest eerily still, that stole the breath from Alex's lungs.

Alex stood frozen, his feet rooted to the pavement. A part of him, the pragmatic part, calculated the minutes ticking away, the ever-growing distance between him and his class. But another part, a more human part, was horrified by the unfolding scene. He wasn't alone in his hesitation. A crowd had gathered, some filming the incident with detached curiosity, others simply looking on with vacant eyes, and some even continuing their day as if nothing had happened.

Just as Alex felt a surge of shame for his inaction, three young men, street artists by the look of their paint-splattered clothes and the ease with which they navigated the urban jungle, burst through the gathering crowd. They moved with a purposefulness that was as mesmerizing as it was galvanizing. One sprinted towards the mangled car, calling out reassurances to the trapped man. Another began directing traffic, his voice booming over the shocked murmurs, creating a safe space for the rescue. The third, with a calmness that belied the chaos, started assessing the damage, his eyes scanning for immediate dangers. Their decisiveness broke the spell that held the crowd captive, and a wave of helping hands, including Alex's, joined the effort.

Alex found himself at the periphery, assisting in clearing debris from the road.  He then joined others in pushing the mangled car away from the main thoroughfare, creating a path for emergency services. By the time the ambulance arrived, the couple was already out of the car, shaken but seemingly unharmed. Relief washed over Alex, but it was quickly followed by a wave of introspection. He had helped, yes, but only after others had taken the initiative. Was it fear that had held him back? Or perhaps a deeper discomfort with being thrust into the spotlight? He wasn't sure, but the feeling left a bitter residue in his mouth.

The wail of sirens jolted him back to reality. His class! With a pang of guilt, he hailed a self-driving motorcycle, knowing the fare would eat into his meager budget. As the motorcycle zipped through the city, his thoughts drifted to the precariousness of his situation. Being from outside the city, surviving on scholarships and odd jobs was a constant struggle. The job market was increasingly filled with short-term gigs, leaving little room for long-term stability. The future he envisioned, the one where he contributed to the very fabric of this magnificent city, felt distant and uncertain.

He arrived at the university just as his class was starting, slipping into the back unnoticed. The lecture was on sustainable urban design, a topic that usually ignited his passion. But today, the images of the accident, the faces of the crowd, and his own hesitant response weighed heavily on his mind. He realized that shaping the future wasn't just about designing buildings; it was about building a better society, one where people stepped up, where apathy was replaced with action, and where everyone, regardless of their background, had a chance to contribute.

"Dude, your head's in the clouds again," Miguel's voice boomed, a playful punch landing on Alex's shoulder. "Professor Mendoza was explaining the whole sustainable drainage system, and you were off in La La Land."

Alex chuckled, rubbing his arm. "Yeah, sorry. My brain was stuck a little."

Miguel, a walking mountain of a man with a heart of gold, was Alex's only real friend in this concrete jungle. Their differences were as stark as the city's contrasts – Miguel, a local with a passion for soccer and the latest comedy shows, and Alex, the outsider, obsessed with architecture and the future of urban design. Yet, somehow, they clicked.

As they exited the university gates and parted ways, the city of Las Flores unfolded before them. The dying sunlight cast long shadows from the towering skyscrapers, their glass facades reflecting the fiery hues of the sky. Below, a chaotic symphony of honking cars, and the rhythmic clatter of the elevated train created a soundtrack that was both jarring and strangely comforting.

A pang of loneliness stabbed at Alex as he looked out at the city sprawling beneath him. The juxtaposition of wealth and poverty, the stark contrast between the gleaming towers and the humble houses clinging to the mountainside, mirrored the conflicting emotions swirling within him. The city, once a symbol of hope and opportunity, now felt like a cold, indifferent entity, reflecting his own feelings of isolation.

He thought back to his conversation with Miguel at the university gates. While Miguel's easy banter and lightheartedness usually provided a welcome distraction, today it had only served to highlight Alex's own introspective mood. Miguel, with his local roots and large family, seemed to effortlessly belong, while Alex felt like a lone satellite, orbiting the periphery of this complex urban system.

The apartment, with its bare walls and quiet neighbors, did little to alleviate his sense of displacement. It was a stark contrast to the warmth and chaos of his childhood home, where the aroma of his mother's cooking had always filled the air. Here, the silence was broken only by the distant hum of the city, a constant reminder of his isolation.

Suddenly, a burst of music erupted from the apartment next door, a lively salsa rhythm that vibrated through the thin walls. Alex felt a flicker of annoyance at first, but then, a reluctant smile crept across his face. It was a sound of life, of connection, of the human spirit refusing to be subdued by the anonymity of the city.

He walked over to his window and leaned out, letting the music wash over him. In the distance, he saw the cable cars gliding across the night sky, like fireflies tracing patterns against the darkness. And for a moment, he felt a sense of belonging, a connection to the vibrant pulse of the city and the people who called it home. Perhaps, he thought, this city wasn't so indifferent after all. Perhaps, amidst the chaos and contrasts, there was a place for him, a chance to build not just structures of concrete and steel, but bridges of understanding and community.

Alex's new apartment building stood in stark contrast to the glittering skyline. It was a squat, concrete block, lost in a maze of similar structures, each window a flickering television screen offering glimpses into the lives of its inhabitants. The lobby smelled of stale cooking and disinfectant, a familiar scent that reminded him of his childhood home in a small coastal town.

His apartment, a cramped studio with peeling paint and a view of a brick wall, was a far cry from the sleek, futuristic dwellings he dreamt of designing. It was a constant reminder of the chasm between his aspirations and his reality. Yet, amidst the chipped tiles and the flickering fluorescent light, Alex found a strange sense of comfort. It was his own little corner in this sprawling metropolis, a place where he could dream of building a better future, one brick at a time.

The rhythmic salsa next door faded into a murmur of voices. Alex leaned further out, catching snippets of conversation from the patio below. An elderly woman lamented the discovery of a deceased neighbor, found alone in his apartment days after his passing. A younger woman chimed in, sharing another recent solitude death, a middle-aged woman just five floors down. A chill snaked down Alex's spine. Two deaths, so close, both alone... it felt like a morbid coincidence, a grim reminder of the city's isolating underbelly.

Unease gnawed at him as he retreated from the window. The city's vibrant pulse now seemed to thrum with a melancholic undertone. The old woman's words echoed in his mind, "Living alone... no one to see them..." It was a reality he'd chosen, sacrificing connection for his ambitions. But the price suddenly felt steeper, the potential isolation at the end of his journey a chilling prospect.

He pictured his future self, successful perhaps, but alone in a sleek, high-rise apartment, his only companions the ghosts of missed connections and unrealized relationships. The image clashed with the warmth of his childhood home, filled with the boisterous laughter of family and the comforting aroma of his mother's cooking.

Shaking off the morbid thoughts, Alex prepped for the next day. Today had been a whirlwind - the near accident, the introspection, the glimpse into the city's darker side. It was a lot to process, a chaotic mix of emotions that mirrored the city itself. He climbed into bed, the salsa beat replaced by the distant hum of the city, a lullaby that now carried a hint of loneliness. But amidst the conflicting feelings, a resolve began to solidify. He would chase his dreams, yes, but not at the cost of human connection. He would find a way to build his future, brick by brick, with room for both ambition and companionship.
