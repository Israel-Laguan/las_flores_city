# Marriage of Carlos Alberto and Elisabeth

> Tags: `#politics` `#dutch_interest` `#corruption`
> 
> **Location:** Las Flores, City District
> **Period:** 2038

## Overview
The marriage of Carlos Alberto Hernandez, the son of the influential politician Carlos Hernandez, to Elisabeth van der Meer, a member of the prominent Dutch family, marked a significant turning point in the political landscape of Las Flores. Initially seen as a strategic alliance between two powerful families, the union had unforeseen consequences that further undermined Carlos Hernandez's political standing.

## Political Consequences
The Chinese community, which had been a major financial and political supporter of Carlos Hernandez, saw the marriage as a betrayal. They viewed the van der Meer family's Dutch roots and their mining interests as a direct threat to their own influence in Las Flores. This perception led to a significant cooling of relations between Carlos Hernandez and his Chinese allies, who had previously been instrumental in funding his political campaigns and ensuring his electoral success.

As the rift between Carlos Hernandez and the Chinese community widened, any prospect of continuing his political career became untenable. The loss of Chinese support, coupled with the growing public scrutiny of his past dealings and allegations of corruption, made it impossible for Carlos Hernandez to seek another term. Faced with the prospect of a bitter and divisive campaign, Carlos Hernandez decided to retire from politics, opting instead to focus on mentoring young leaders and engaging in philanthropic work.

## Family Dynamics
The marriage between Carlos Alberto and Elisabeth was further complicated by the uncomfortable dynamics within the Hernandez family. Carlos Hernandez, the father, had a reputation for being somewhat inappropriate in his interactions with Elisabeth. His advances were subtle but unmistakable, making Elisabeth increasingly uncomfortable around him.

Elisabeth's mother-in-law, a submissive woman accustomed to the patriarchal norms of the Hernandez household, attempted to instruct Elisabeth on how to behave in a more traditional and motherly manner towards Carlos Alberto. This advice clashed with Elisabeth's European upbringing and her independent spirit, creating a tense atmosphere within the family.

Carlos Alberto maintained a close relationship with his parents, often seeking their advice and approval. This closeness was a source of constant frustration for Elisabeth, who found herself continually battling to assert her own influence over her husband. She felt that Carlos Alberto was too dependent on his parents and lacked the independence and critical thinking necessary for a successful politician.

## Corruption
Elisabeth struggled to break this cycle, encouraging Carlos Alberto to make his own decisions and form his own opinions. However, her efforts were often met with resistance from Carlos Alberto, who was deeply entrenched in the familial dynamics and the political legacy of his father.

Carlos Alberto's Political Alignment and Descent into Corruption
Carlos Alberto was initially drawn to the Economic Libertarian Union (ELU), the political party of his father, which had strong ties with the Chinese community. This alignment was influenced by his father's political legacy and the allure of power and wealth that the ELU promised. However, Elisabeth had hoped that Carlos Alberto would align with the Partido Popular de Las Flores (PLF), which was more supportive of Latin American interests and social justice.

Within the ELU, Carlos Alberto was quickly influenced by other corrupt politicians. He was introduced to a world of lavish parties, exclusive travels, and luxurious lifestyles, all funded by illicit transactions and bribes. The allure of this lifestyle was too great for Carlos Alberto to resist, and he soon found himself deeply entrenched in the corrupt practices of the ELU.

Elisabeth, while initially enjoying the luxury and high-end restaurants that came with the corrupt lifestyle, grew increasingly uncomfortable with the presence of Chinese business interests in their lives. She was torn between her desire for material wealth and her disdain for the corrupt practices that funded it.

Elisabeth maintained close ties with her Dutch family, who visited their house regularly. These visits provided a sense of comfort and familiarity for Elisabeth, but they also highlighted the growing divide between her and Carlos Alberto. The Dutch family, with their strong values and independent spirit, never truly clicked with Carlos Alberto, who was deeply entrenched in the corrupt political culture of the ELU.
