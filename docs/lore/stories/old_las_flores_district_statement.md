# Official Statement from the District of Old Las Flores on Efforts to Curb Insecurity and Social Unrest

> Tags: `#story` `#government` `#old_las_flores` `#insecurity` `#gang_violence`
>
> **Source:** District of Old Las Flores (official statement)

## Overview

An official statement outlining the District of Old Las Flores' commitment to addressing gang violence and social unrest that has plagued the historic neighborhood amid the city's broader crises.

---

## Four-Pillar Strategy

1. **Strengthened Police Presence**
   - Increased patrols in high-risk areas
   - Dedicated community policing unit to foster better resident-law enforcement relationships

2. **Community Engagement**
   - Neighborhood clean-up campaigns
   - Public safety workshops
   - Community events bringing together local organizations, businesses, and families

3. **Youth Programming**
   - Expanded after-school programs
   - Sports leagues
   - Mentorship opportunities keeping young people engaged positively

4. **Partnerships with Non-profits and NGOs**
   - Strategic partnerships leveraging expertise to address root causes of gang violence: poverty, lack of education, limited economic opportunities

## Key Priorities

| Priority | Action |
|---|---|
| **Targeted Interventions** | Identify and dismantle criminal networks; provide rehabilitation and reintegration support |
| **Infrastructure Development** | Invest in public spaces (parks, community centers) as hubs for positive activity |
| **Economic Development** | Stimulate growth and job creation with local businesses and investors |
| **Education & Workforce Development** | Expand access to quality education and skills training, including partnerships with Global Lithium Corp |

## Context

Old Las Flores — a historic hub for the city's immigrant communities — struggles with high unemployment, decaying infrastructure, and an alarming rise in gang-related violence. Residents feel their concerns are falling on deaf ears at City Hall.
