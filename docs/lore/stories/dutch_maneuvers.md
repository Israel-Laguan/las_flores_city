# The Dutch Maneuvers to Outsmart the Chinese

> Tags: `#dutch_interest` `#politics` `#constitution`
> 
> **Location:** Las Flores city government
> **Period:** ~2030–2036

## Overview
Initial Observations and Concerns
In 2030, the Dutch mining consortium led by Pieter van der Meer began to notice the growing Chinese influence over the port in Las Flores. The Chinese, with their larger mine and substantial investments, were profiting significantly from their operations. Pieter and his team realized that over time, the Dutch operations could be absorbed by the Chinese, jeopardizing their long-term interests in the region. By 2033, the discovery of an additional lithium vein by Van der Meer strengthened their bargaining position and accelerated the free city initiative.

## Strategic Planning
Recognizing the need to equalize the power balance and secure their position, Pieter devised a plan to create a new political entity in Las Flores — one that would be less influenced by the Chinese. This entity, a "free city," would attract more foreign investment and provide the Dutch with greater leverage.

## Gathering Support
Pieter began by approaching local businesspeople and investors who shared his concerns about the Chinese dominance. He found a key ally in Juan Pablo Ramos, a charismatic and influential local businessman who had strong connections within the community. Together, they formed a coalition of local and international investors who saw the benefits of creating a free city.

## Political Influence
Pieter and Juan Pablo recognized the importance of political support. They approached Mayor Hernandez, offering him the prospect of boosting his re-election prospects by supporting the free city initiative. The mayor, seeing the potential for personal gain and increased investment in the city, agreed to back the proposal.

## Bribing and Persuasion
To ensure widespread support, Pieter and Juan Pablo employed a combination of persuasion and bribery. They offered financial incentives to key local officials, business leaders, and community influencers to garner their backing. This strategy was crucial in building a broad coalition that could push the initiative forward.

## Chinese Negotiations
Pieter understood that the Chinese could not be entirely sidelined. He approached the Chinese representatives with a cleverly crafted proposal. Presenting the free city initiative as a mutually beneficial plan, Pieter argued that increased foreign investment would boost the overall economy, benefiting both the Dutch and the Chinese.

In the secret negotiations with the Chinese, the Chinese representatives, led by Li Wei, asked for more than half of the influence on the city. This demand was part of their strategy to consolidate their control over Las Flores and secure their economic interests in the region.

Pieter van der Meer, recognizing the strategic importance of the city, pushed to negotiate control over the City District. However, his primary interest lay in securing control over the port and establishing a governance model that would favor the Dutch and Latin American populations.

The port was a critical point of contention. Pieter proposed that the Dutch be given significant control over the port to facilitate their lithium exports. However, the Chinese were unwilling to relinquish their hold on the port. After lengthy discussions, the Chinese agreed to allow an independent pier for anyone, but only granted the Dutch a limited space on the row of disembarks. This compromise allowed the Dutch to have some independence in their operations but fell short of their initial goals.

Pieter's plan for the governance model was to divide the city as much as possible and base voting power on population numbers. He understood that the Dutch and Latin American populations could counter the Chinese influence through sheer numbers.

To get the deal, the Dutch had to make significant concessions. They agreed to give half of the airport and the Luz del Rio area to the Chinese. Additionally, more than half of the city, specifically the northern part, was to be under Chinese control. In return, the southern part of the city would be managed by the Dutch and Latin American interests.

## The Referendum
In 2033, a proposal for the Free City of Las Flores was formally presented. The actual constitutional referendum took place in **2036**, when the measure passed with 65% of voters approving the creation of the free city amid allegations of voter intimidation and fraud. Juan Pablo Ramos assumed the role of Governor, formalizing a leadership position he had held provisionally since 2033. Mayor Carlos Hernandez transitioned to become the first **Mayor of the City District**, governing the administrative core while the constitution was being finalized.
