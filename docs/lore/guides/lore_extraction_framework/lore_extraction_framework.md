## ✍️ WRITING STYLE EXTRACTION

### First-Person Narrative Framework

**Core Characteristics:**
1. **Perspective:** First-person pronouns (I, me, my)
2. **Tone:** Conversational, intimate
3. **Structure:** Stream of consciousness
4. **Detail:** Vivid, sensory descriptions
5. **Depth:** Internal monologue, emotional responses
6. **Language:** Informal, colloquial, natural speech
7. **Tense:** Present tense (immediacy)

### Style Application by Medium

#### For Lore Entries
Use first-person for:
- Character diaries/journals
- Personal testimonies
- Internal monologues
- Stream-of-consciousness revelations

**Example Structure:**
```markdown
> I'm standing on the rooftop, the city spread out below me.
> The lights from Mercado Central pulse like a heartbeat, but I know 
> what's beneath that glow. Carlos knew too. That's why he's not here 
> anymore. 
> 
> I can feel the vibration of the HVAC system through my boots. 
> Something's wrong. I don't need Miguel's spreadsheets to tell me that.
```

#### For UI Text
First-person works for:
- Character thoughts in overlays
- Journal entries
- Internal notifications
- Personal quest descriptions

#### For Prompts
Use first-person perspective in character portraits:
- "I am a 24-year-old electrical engineering student"
- "I work part-time at an electronics repair shop"
- "I say yes before you finish the sentence"

## 🔄 USAGE WORKFLOW

### For Lore Development

1. **Start with Setting:** Use the city description and locations
2. **Add Characters:** Place them in appropriate locations
3. **Develop Relationships:** Use the group dynamics framework
4. **Create Conflicts:** Apply the socioeconomic and political themes
5. **Write in Style:** Use first-person narrative where appropriate

### For UI/UX Design

1. **Choose Color Palette:** Based on location or character
2. **Select Visual Motifs:** Match the themes being presented
3. **Design Components:** Use the component library
4. **Apply Layout Principles:** Contrast and duality
5. **Test with Content:** Real lore entries in the design

### For Asset Generation

1. **Select Prompt Type:** Location, character, or scene
2. **Customize Elements:** Adjust based on specific needs
3. **Choose Style:** Realistic, cyberpunk, cinematic, etc.
4. **Set Parameters:** Resolution, lighting, mood
5. **Generate & Iterate:** Refine based on results
