### 🔹 TEMPLATE 1: QUICK LORE ENTRY

```markdown
### [Name]
**Type:** [Character/Location/Event/Object/Faction]
**Thematic Role:** [How it reinforces the world's core contrasts]

#### 📌 [Category] Details
- **[Detail 1]**: [Description]
- **[Detail 2]**: [Description]
- **[Detail 3]**: [Description]

#### 👥 Social Details
- **Connected Characters:** [Who interacts with it?]
- **Power Dynamics:** [Who controls it? Who resists?]
- **Secrets:** [Hidden truths or corruption]

#### 💭 Thematic Details
- **Symbolizes:** [What it represents (e.g., hope, decay, resistance)]
- **Contrasts With:** [Opposing element in the world]
- **Character Arc Impact:** [How it changes people]

#### 📜 Expanded Lore (Optional)
- **Backstory:** [How it came to be]
- **2077:** [Role during the movement]
- **Post-2077:** [Where it's headed after the climax]
- **Quote/Mantra:** [Key dialogue or sayings associated with it]

#### 🔗 Connections
- [[Related Lore 1]]
- [[Related Lore 2]]
- [[Related Lore 3]]
```

**Example (Location):**
```markdown
### The Garden of Lies
**Type:** Location
**Thematic Role:** Beauty masking corruption – A place where the elite pretend to care about nature while destroying it.

#### 📌 Physical Details
- **Appearance:** A rooftop garden atop the Vega Tower, with withered plants and contaminated soil.
- **Sensory Details:** The scent of decay beneath the perfume of flowers; the hum of the city below.
- **Key Features:** String lights, view of poor districts, Andean foothills in the distance.

#### 👥 Social Details
- **Connected Characters:** Mayor Vega (owner), Isabella (infiltrates it), Miguel (has a contact inside)
- **Power Dynamics:** LW Group uses it for secret meetings; the poor districts see it as a symbol of hypocrisy.
- **Secrets:** Hidden microphones (Isabella planted them); contaminated soil from Luz del Río's waste.

#### 💭 Thematic Details
- **Symbolizes:** The hollow gestures of the elite.
- **Contrasts With:** Parque de las Montañas (a real natural space).
- **Character Arc Impact:** Isabella records a confession here (Mayor Vega admitting to bribes).

#### 📜 Expanded Lore
- **Backstory:** Built in 2070 as a "green initiative" by Mayor Vega.
- **2077:** Used for secret meetings during the movement.
- **Post-2077:** Abandoned – the plants never recovered.
- **Quote:** "The mayor's garden is as alive as his conscience." – Graffiti on the tower's base.

#### 🔗 Connections
- [[Mayor Vega]]
- [[Isabella Vargas]]
- [[LW Group]]
```

**Example (Character):**
```markdown
### Javier "El Fantasma" Morales
**Role:** The Shadow (Smuggler, Informant)
**Age (2077):** 30
**Background:** Grew up in the eastern district; lost his brother to LW Group enforcers in 2075.

#### Who He Is
Javier is a ghost in the machine—a smuggler who knows every back alley, hidden tunnel, and corrupt official in Las Flores. He's lean, wiry, and moves like a shadow, with a scar across his left cheek (a reminder of his brother's death). He speaks in short, clipped sentences and always has an exit plan.

#### Their Arc
- **Pre-2077:** Runs black market goods for the poor districts.
- **2077:** Joins the movement after Isabella promises to help take down LW Group.
- **Post-2077:** Disappears—rumored to be working with Evelyn's shadow network.

#### Their Cost
- **Physical:** Scar from a knife fight.
- **Emotional:** Survivor's guilt over his brother's death.
- **Social:** Wanted by LW Group for betrayal.
- **Moral:** Smuggles weapons for the movement (haunts him).

#### Connections
- **Isabella Vargas:** Main contact in the movement.
- **Miguel Jhonson:** Uses his logistics network to move goods.
- **Eastern District:** Home turf; knows every inch.
- **LW Group:** Wants him dead for betraying them.

#### Visual Cues
- Lean, wiry build
- Scar across left cheek
- Dark circles under eyes
- Worn leather jacket, cap, fingerless gloves
- Switchblade (never used), burner phones
- **Expression:** Calculating, tense

#### Quote
> "I don't do this for free. But for them? For what they did to my brother? That's on the house."

#### Tags
> `#figure` `#shadow` `#eastern_district` `#criminal_underworld`
```

**Example (Event):**
```markdown
### The Blackout
**Type:** Confrontation
**When:** 3 months before Carlos's death (Early 2077)
**Where:** Western District (Miguel's neighborhood)
**Key Characters:** Miguel Jhonson, Javier Morales, Western District Residents

#### 🔥 Trigger
LW Group cuts power to the western district to pressure residents into accepting a new pipeline for Luz del Río's expansion.

#### ⚖️ Choices
| **Option** | **Proposed By** | **Risk** | **Reward** |
|------------|-----------------|----------|-----------|
| Protest | Community Leaders | Police crackdown | Public solidarity |
| Sabotage | Javier Morales | LW Group retaliation | Immediate power restoration |
| Black Market Grid | Miguel Jhonson | Labeled a terrorist | Sustainable solution |
**Chosen Path:** Miguel organizes a black market power grid using stolen tech from Luz del Río (via Javier's contacts).

#### 💀 Cost
- **Physical:** A worker (Ricardo Chen) is electrocuted setting up the grid.
- **Emotional:** Miguel carries guilt over Ricardo's death.
- **Social:** LW Group labels Miguel a terrorist; his family is harassed.
- **Moral:** Miguel must work with Javier, a known smuggler.

#### 💡 Revelation
The group learns Luz del Río is overcharging the city for electricity—proof of corruption that can be used against them.

#### 🌊 Ripple Effects
- **Immediate:** Western district unites against LW Group; protests erupt.
- **Long-Term:** Miguel's logistics network (for the movement) is born here; Javier gains Miguel's trust.

#### 📎 Connected Lore
- [[Miguel Jhonson]]
- [[Javier Morales]]
- [[Luz del Río Energy Plant]]
- [[LW Group]]
- [[Western District]]

#### 📝 Notes
This event is the first time Miguel and Javier work together, laying the groundwork for their partnership during the 2077 movement. Ricardo Chen's death foreshadows Carlos's later sacrifice.
```

**Example (Faction):**
```markdown
### Los Hijos del Río (River's Children)
**Type:** Activist Group
**Status (2077):** Active (Underground)
**Leadership:** Maria Vasquez (Carlos Lacan's sister)
**Headquarters:** Hidden network of safehouses along the Río de las Flores

#### 🎯 Goals
- Clean the Río de las Flores and restore it to its natural state.
- Expose LW Group's environmental crimes.
- Protect the poor districts from industrial exploitation.

#### 💼 Operations
- **Public Face:** Environmental awareness campaigns, community clean-up events.
- **Secret Activities:** Sabotage of LW Group pipelines, theft of incriminating documents, smuggling of evidence to journalists.
- **Methods:** Non-violent direct action, whistleblowing, community organizing.

#### 👥 Membership
- **Size:** ~50 core members, hundreds of supporters
- **Demographics:** Primarily poor and middle-class residents of the western and northern districts; many have lost family to LW Group's pollution.
- **Recruitment:** Word of mouth, community meetings, personal connections.

#### 🏛️ Power & Influence
- **Allies:** Miguel Jhonson's logistics network, Isabella Vargas (intelligence), El Informador (media).
- **Enemies:** LW Group, Mayor Vega, local police.
- **Resources:** Hidden caches of documents, safehouses, community support.

#### 💀 Notable Actions
- **2070-2076:** Organized protests against Luz del Río's expansion; several members arrested.
- **2077:** Provided crucial evidence about LW Group's illegal dumping into the river (used in Alex's broadcast).
- **Post-2077:** Maria Vasquez arrested in 2078; group continues without her.

#### 🔗 Connections
- [[Carlos Lacan]] (Inspiration; Maria is his sister)
- [[Miguel Jhonson]] (Logistics support)
- [[Isabella Vargas]] (Intelligence sharing)
- [[Río de las Flores]] (Primary focus)
- [[The Blackout]] (First major action)

#### 📜 Quote
> "The river remembers. And so do we."
```

## ✅ QUALITY CONTROL CHECKLISTS

*Use these to ensure all content meets Las Flores standards.*

---

### 🔹 FOR LORE ENTRIES

- [ ] **🔹 Duality Check:** Does it reflect **at least one core contrast** (Beauty/Corruption, Nature/Industry, Wealth/Poverty, etc.)?
- [ ] **🔗 Connection Check:** Is it linked to **2+ existing lore elements** (characters, locations, factions, events)?
- [ ] **💭 Human Stakes:** Does it have a **personal cost or emotional weight**?
- [ ] **📜 Consistency Check:** Does it **align with the timeline** (Pre/During/Post-2077)?
- [ ] **🌍 Cultural Check:** Does it reflect **Las Flores' multiculturalism** (surnames, heritage, diversity)?
- [ ] **🤖 Tech Check:** Is the **technology level** appropriate (soft cyberpunk, no androids)?
- [ ] **📝 Language Check:** Matches the **first-person, present-tense style** (for narratives)?
- [ ] **🎯 Thematic Check:** Does it reinforce the **core themes** of the world?

---

### 🔹 FOR UI/UX DESIGN

- [ ] **🎨 Visual Identity:** Uses the **color palette, textures, and fonts** from the [Design System](../ui_ux_design_system.md)?
- [ ] **🧭 Navigation:** Is it **intuitive** and **linked to other relevant pages**?
- [ ] **📖 Lore Integration:** Does it **showcase the duality** of Las Flores?
- [ ] **📱 Responsive:** Does it work on **mobile, tablet, and desktop**?
- [ ] **♿ Accessibility:** Are **colors, text, and interactions** accessible? (Use [axe DevTools](https://www.deque.com/axe/))
- [ ] **⚡ Performance:** Are **images optimized** (not too large)? Are **assets lazy-loaded**?
- [ ] **🎭 Mood:** Does the design **match the tone** of the location/character?
- [ ] **🔗 Cross-Linking:** Are there **clear paths** to related content?

---

### 🔹 FOR IMAGES & VIDEOS

- [ ] **📝 Prompt Check:** Does it include **style, setting, mood, lighting, composition, details, contrast**?
- [ ] **❌ Negative Prompt Check:** Are **unwanted elements excluded**?
- [ ] **🎯 Consistency Check:** Does it **match the lore** (e.g., no neon if it's not in the description)?
- [ ] **🖼️ Quality Check:** Is it **high-resolution (minimum 2K)** and **detailed**?
- [ ] **😢 Emotion Check:** Does it **convey the right mood** (tense, hopeful, melancholic, etc.)?
- [ ] **🌈 Contrast Check:** Does it **visually represent the duality**?
- [ ] **👤 Character Accuracy:** Do the characters **match their descriptions**?
- [ ] **🏙️ Location Accuracy:** Does the setting **match the lore**?

---

### 🔹 FOR NEW CHARACTERS

- [ ] **🎭 Role Defined:** Do they fit **one of the 6 archetypes**?
- [ ] **💀 Cost Paid:** Do they have a **physical, emotional, or moral price**?
- [ ] **🔗 Connections:** Are they **linked to 2+ existing characters/locations**?
- [ ] **👕 Visual Cues:** Are there **3+ traits** for artists to reference?
- [ ] **🌍 Cultural Fit:** Does their **name/background** reflect Las Flores' multiculturalism?
- [ ] **📜 Arc Defined:** Do they have a **clear beginning, middle, and end**?
- [ ] **🎨 Portrait Generated:** Is there a **reference image** for the character?

---

### 🔹 FOR EVENTS

- [ ] **📅 Timeline Placement:** Is the **date/time** clear and consistent?
- [ ] **🔥 Trigger Defined:** Is the **spark** for the event clear?
- [ ] **⚖️ Choices Explored:** Are there **multiple options** the characters consider?
- [ ] **💀 Cost Identified:** Do the characters **lose something**?
- [ ] **💡 Revelation:** Do they **learn something** that changes the story?
- [ ] **🌊 Ripple Effects:** Does it **impact the world** in a lasting way?
- [ ] **📎 Connected Lore:** Is it **linked to existing characters/locations**?
