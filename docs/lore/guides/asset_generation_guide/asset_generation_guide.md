## 🔹 CORE PRINCIPLES

*Follow these to maintain consistency across all assets.*

| **Principle** | **Application** | **Example** |
|--------------|----------------|-------------|
| **🔹 Duality First** | Every element must reflect **at least one contrast** (beauty/corruption, nature/industry, wealth/poverty). | Luz del Río = progress **and** destruction. |
| **🔹 Multicultural Roots** | Surnames, architecture, and cultural details must reflect the **diaspora** (Europe, Asia, Africa, Latin America). | **Kim (Asian), Jhonson (Anglo-Caribbean), Lacan (French-Caribbean), Vargas (Latin American)**. |
| **🔹 Soft Cyberpunk** | Tech exists (AI, automation) but **no androids, no extreme violence**. Focus on **social displacement**. | Factories with AI workers, but no robot uprisings. |
| **🔹 Environmental Storytelling** | The **setting itself tells the story**. Contamination, architecture, and geography are **active characters**. | The river's pollution = the city's moral decay. |
| **🔹 Human Scale** | Even in grand scenes, **focus on personal stakes**. The movement succeeds because of **individual choices**. | Carlos's death changes Alex's risk tolerance. |
| **🔹 Post-2077 Hope** | The transitional era is **not utopian**—it's **fragile, hard-won, and imperfect**. | Miguel rebuilds housing policy, but it's slow, unglamorous work. |

## 📖 LORE GENERATION FRAMEWORK

*How to create new stories, characters, and world details that fit Las Flores.*

---

### 🔹 1. THE "3-LAYER" LORE MODEL

Every piece of lore should connect to **at least 2 of these layers**:

| **Layer** | **Definition** | **Examples** | **How to Use** |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|---------------|
| **🏙️ Physical** | Geography, architecture, objects | Río de las Flores, Luz del Río, cableways, faded buildings | *Describe the environment first—it drives the story.* |
| **👥 Social** | People, relationships, power | Miguel's logistics network, Ana's library access, Isabella's spreadsheet | *Who knows whom? Who has leverage?* |
| **💭 Thematic** | Ideas, conflicts, symbols | Beauty vs. corruption, AI displacement, environmental justice | *What does this **mean** in the world?* |

**Example: The Apartment Building**
- **Physical**: Rounded, faded architecture; microcosm of class collision.
- **Social**: Hunters (Isabella) and hunted (corrupt officials) live side by side.
- **Thematic**: The eroding community (faded colors) vs. elite control (stark designs).

---

### 🔹 2. THE "5 Ws + H" LORE TEMPLATE

*For generating new characters, locations, or events.*

```markdown
### [Name]
**Type:** [Character/Location/Event/Object]
**Thematic Role:** [How it reinforces the world's core contrasts]

#### 📌 Physical Details
- **Appearance:** [Visual traits]
- **Location:** [Where it exists in the city]
- **Sensory Details:** [Smells, sounds, textures]

#### 👥 Social Details
- **Connected Characters:** [Who interacts with it?]
- **Power Dynamics:** [Who controls it? Who resists?]
- **Secrets:** [Hidden truths or corruption]

#### 💭 Thematic Details
- **Symbolizes:** [What it represents (e.g., hope, decay, resistance)]
- **Contrasts With:** [Opposing element in the world]
- **Character Arc Impact:** [How it changes people]

#### 📜 Expanded Lore (Optional)
- **Backstory:** [How it came to be]
- **Future:** [Where it's headed post-2077]
- **Quotes/Mantras:** [Key dialogue or sayings associated with it]
```

### 🔹 3. CHARACTER CREATION GUIDE

*How to invent new figures that fit the group dynamic.*

#### Step 1: Pick a Role in the Movement

| **Role** | **Function** | **Example from Core Group** | **New Character Idea** |
|----------|-------------|-----------------------------|-------------------------|
| **The Spark** | Starts the action, inspires others | Alex | Sofia Mendez – Journalist who uncovers the first corruption story. |
| **The Anchor** | Logistics, stability | Miguel | Rafael "Rafe" Okafor – Former delivery driver, knows every back road. |
| **The Heart** | Emotional core, morale booster | Carlos | Lena Chen – Medic who patches up the group, always optimistic. |
| **The Brain** | Intelligence, analysis | Isabella | Dr. Elias Kabeer – Retired professor, deciphers financial documents. |
| **The Conscience** | Ethical limits, voice of reason | Ana | Father Mateo – Priest who shelters dissidents, struggles with violence. |
| **The Shadow** | Hidden operations, risk-taker | Evelyn | Javier "El Fantasma" Morales – Smuggler turned informant. |

#### Step 2: Define Their "Cost"

*Every character in Las Flores **pays a price** for their involvement.*
- **Physical:** Injury (Ana's bruised ribs), death (Carlos).
- **Emotional:** Guilt (Miguel's precision with others' lives), trauma (Isabella's uncle's case).
- **Social:** Stigma (poor/middle class labeled "delinquents"), isolation (Ana leaving the group).
- **Moral:** Compromise (Isabella knocking out the guard), secrets (Miguel's unspoken final minutes with Alex).

#### Step 3: Connect to Existing Lore

- **Family Ties:** Are they related to someone in the core group? (Isabella's uncle = similar death to Las Estrellas victims.)
- **Location:** Where do they live/work? (Ana = university library; Miguel = western district.)
- **Skills:** What do they bring? (Carlos = technical; Isabella = pattern recognition.)
- **Flaws:** What's their limitation? (Ana = can't sustain high risk; Miguel = over-protective.)

**Example: New Character – "Javier 'El Fantasma' Morales"**

```markdown
### Javier "El Fantasma" Morales
**Role:** The Shadow (Smuggler, Informant)
**Age (2077):** 30
**Background:** Grew up in the eastern district; lost his brother to LW Group enforcers in 2075.

#### Who He Is
Javier is a **ghost in the machine**—a smuggler who knows every back alley, hidden tunnel, and corrupt official in Las Flores. He's **lean, wiry, and moves like a shadow**, with a scar across his left cheek (a reminder of his brother's death). He speaks in **short, clipped sentences** and always has an exit plan.

#### His Arc
- **Pre-2077:** Runs **black market goods** for the poor districts.
- **2077:** Joins the movement after **Isabella promises to help take down LW Group**.
- **Post-2077:** **Disappears**—rumored to be working with Evelyn's shadow network.

#### His Cost
- **Physical:** Scar from a knife fight.
- **Emotional:** **Survivor's guilt** over his brother's death.
- **Moral:** **Smuggles weapons** for the movement (haunts him).

#### Connections
- **Isabella:** His **main contact** in the movement.
- **Miguel:** Uses his **logistics network** to move goods.
- **LW Group:** **Wants him dead** for betraying them.

#### Visual Cues
- **Clothing:** Worn leather jacket, cap, fingerless gloves.
- **Accessories:** **Switchblade** (never used), **burner phones**.
- **Location:** **Eastern district alleys**, docks.

#### Quote
*"I don't do this for free. But for them? For what they did to my brother? That's on the house."*
```

### 🔹 4. EVENT CREATION GUIDE

*How to design new story beats that feel organic.*

#### The "Cause → Effect → Consequence" Chain

Every major event should have:
1. **A Trigger** (What sets it in motion?)
2. **A Choice** (What do the characters decide?)
3. **A Cost** (What do they lose?)
4. **A Revelation** (What do they learn?)
5. **A Ripple** (How does it affect the world?)

**Example: The City Hall Archive Break-In**
1. **Trigger:** Passive surveillance (cameras) yields **no results**.
2. **Choice:** Isabella **proposes the break-in**; group debates risks.
3. **Cost:** Ana is **injured** (bruised ribs, swollen eye).
4. **Revelation:** They find **bribes, contaminated land approvals, Mayor Vega's ties to LW Group**.
5. **Ripple:** Ana **leaves the group**; Isabella becomes the **intelligence anchor**.

**Template for New Events:**

```markdown
### [Event Name]
**Type:** [Investigation/Confrontation/Discovery/Betrayal]
**When:** [Timeline placement – Pre-2077/During 2077/Post-2077]
**Where:** [Location]

#### 🔥 Trigger
- [What sparks the event?]

#### ⚖️ Choices
- **Option A:** [What one faction wants]
- **Option B:** [What another wants]
- **Chosen Path:** [What happens]

#### 💀 Cost
- [Physical/Emotional/Social price paid]

#### 💡 Revelation
- [What the characters learn]

#### 🌊 Ripple Effects
- **Immediate:** [Short-term impact]
- **Long-Term:** [Lasting change in the world]
```

**Example: New Event – "The Blackout"**

```markdown
### The Blackout
**Type:** Confrontation
**When:** 3 months before Carlos's death
**Where:** Western District (Miguel's neighborhood)

#### 🔥 Trigger
LW Group **cuts power** to the western district to **pressure residents** into accepting a new pipeline.

#### ⚖️ Choices
- **Option A:** **Protest** (risk police crackdown).
- **Option B:** **Sabotage** (risk LW Group retaliation).
- **Chosen Path:** Miguel organizes a **black market power grid** (using stolen tech from Luz del Río).

#### 💀 Cost
- **Physical:** A **worker is electrocuted** setting up the grid.
- **Social:** LW Group **labels Miguel a terrorist**.

#### 💡 Revelation
- The group learns **Luz del Río is overcharging the city** → **proof of corruption**.

#### 🌊 Ripple Effects
- **Immediate:** Western district **unites** against LW Group.
- **Long-Term:** Miguel's **logistics network** (for the movement) is **born here**.
```

### 🔹 5. LORE CONNECTION MAP

*How to link new content to existing lore.*

**Use this flowchart to ensure new additions **reinforce** the world:**

```
[New Idea]
   │
   ▼
┌─────────────────────┐
│ Does it reflect      │──── NO ──► Rework
│ a CORE CONTRADICTION?│
│ (Beauty/Corruption,  │
│  Nature/Industry,    │
│  Wealth/Poverty)     │
└──────────┬──────────┘
           │ YES
           ▼
┌─────────────────────┐
│ Does it connect to   │──── NO ──► Add a link
│ EXISTING LORE?        │
│ (Characters, Locations,│
│  Themes, Timeline)    │
└──────────┬──────────┘
           │ YES
           ▼
┌─────────────────────┐
│ Does it have a       │──── NO ──► Add depth
│ HUMAN STAKE?          │
│ (Personal cost,      │
│  emotional weight)   │
└──────────┬──────────┘
           │ YES
           ▼
       [APPROVED]
```

**Example: Adding a New Faction – "Los Hijos del Río" (River's Children)**
1. **Core Contradiction?** YES – They **fight to clean the river** (beauty) vs. **LW Group's pollution** (corruption).
2. **Existing Lore Connection?** YES – They're **led by Carlos's sister** (connects to his legacy).
3. **Human Stake?** YES – Their **leader is arrested** in 2078, forcing Miguel to **negotiate with LW Group**.
