# Transportation in Las Flores

> Tags: `#transportation` `#infrastructure` `#metro` `#buses` `#streets` `#urban_design`

## Overview

Las Flores' meticulously planned streets, zones, and transportation networks illustrate a city designed for both functionality and beauty. The road and public transit systems ensure every part of the city is accessible, facilitating economic activity, cultural engagement, and leisure. The thoughtful layout not only supports the daily needs of residents but also encourages exploration and enjoyment of the city's diverse offerings.

---

## Metro de Las Flores (MLF)

The metro system comprises three lines that intersect at key points, offering efficient travel across the city.

### Lines

| Line | Route | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Line 1 (Red)** | North-South | Paralleling Avenida de las Naciones, linking the mountains to the valley |
| **Line 2 (Blue)** | East-West | Following Avenida Independencia, connecting recreational and residential areas |
| **Line 3 (Green)** | Diagonal | Complementing Bulevar Progreso and Avenida Esperanza for comprehensive coverage |

## Design Considerations

### Pedestrian Zones
Areas around cultural landmarks, such as Teatro Nacional and La Casa de la MÃšsica, are designated as pedestrian zones, encouraging foot traffic and reducing vehicular congestion in the city center.

### Bicycle Lanes
Along all major calles and avenues, dedicated bicycle lanes promote cycling as a preferred mode of transportation for short distances, contributing to a reduction in central city crowding.

### Public Transportation Priority
Both bus and train stations are integrated with major destinations like the financial district and Universidad Nacional, ensuring that public transport is the most convenient option, thereby minimizing private vehicle use in the city center.
