## Overview

Nestled in the rugged embrace of the Andean foothills lies the Minera Estrella Andes Mine, a testament to human ingenuity and perseverance. Located just 5 kilometers northeast of the river, this mine leverages its strategic position to tap into the mountain's generous mineral deposits, primarily lithium, while utilizing the river to streamline the transportation of materials. As the oldest and largest lithium mine in the region, Minera Estrella Andes carries with it a legacy that spans around half a century.

For more information about the mining company, see: [Minera Estrella](../../../organizations/companies/minera_estrella/minera_estrella.md)

For more information about the surrounding district, see: [Los Andes District](../../los_andes.md)

- **River Proximity:** 5 kilometers northeast of the river, enabling efficient material transportation
- **Initial Operations:** Began as a modest extraction site that grew into a major industrial operation

### Economic Significance
- **Primary Output:** Lithium, a critical mineral for modern technology and energy industries
- **Regional Importance:** Largest lithium mine in the area, supplying global markets
- **Economic Driver:** Major contributor to Las Flores' economy and industrial development

### Milestones
- **Early Years:** Modest production with manual extraction methods
- **Expansion:** Growth into large-scale industrial operations
- **Modern Era:** Integration of advanced technologies and automation

### The Great Lithium Leak
- **Date:** August 15, 2052
- **Location:** Container facility 10km from the mine (not at the mine itself)
- **Impact:** Catastrophic release of lithium hydroxide into RĂ­o de las Flores
- **Consequences:** Hundreds of deaths, massive environmental damage, long-term contamination
- **Aftermath:** Cover-up attempts, legal proceedings, and ongoing cleanup efforts
