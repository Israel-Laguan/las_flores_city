A sprawling labyrinth of concrete and steel, the Industrial Zone lies sandwiched between the central city area in the north and Old Las Flores in the south, with the Pacific coast on the west. This region bears the marks of rapid industrialization and urbanization, a stark contrast from its humble beginnings as housing and fincas around Old Las Flores.

---

## Geography & Character

The district is a dense mix of factories, workshops, warehouses, and residential blocks. Streets are a hive of activity with carpentries, metal works, car repairs, furniture stores, and virtually every type of industry imaginable. Delivery trucks, motorbikes, bicycles, and cars contribute to the bustling atmosphere. Restaurants and stores add to the commercial vibrancy, but the zone's edgier side is apparent in abandoned buildings and green areas occupied by street inhabitants or under the control of local gangs.

## Demographics & Economy

The district is predominantly blue-collar, home to factory workers, mechanics, artisans, and small business owners. As the wealthy moved north over time, these neighborhoods became central to the working-class community of Las Flores.

**Economic Activities:**
- Manufacturing and light industry
- Metalwork, carpentry, furniture production
- Automotive repair and maintenance
- Street vending and local markets
- **Illicit:** Drug trafficking, extortion, cybercrime, illegal transport

---

## Criminal Presence

The Industrial Zone is the heart of Las Flores' underworld:

- **Flowers Syndicate:** Led by Dong van der Meer, controls drug trafficking, extortion, and cybercrime operations
- **Smaller Gangs:** Fragmented control over specific neighborhoods and abandoned areas
- **Safety:** Certain areas become perilous after dark; abandoned buildings serve as gang hideouts

---

## Transportation

The district's strategic location makes it a hub for the movement of goods and people:

- **Roads:** Wide streets, heavy truck traffic connecting to CLF ring road
- **Proximity:** Connects Central District, Port, Airport, and Old Las Flores
- **Public Transit:** Bus routes serving the working-class population
