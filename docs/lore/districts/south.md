## Geography & Character

The South District is predominantly rural, characterized by agricultural communities and small hills that contour the fertile valleys. The RĂ­o de las Flores meanders from the eastern mountains through the district's expanse, nurturing the land and supporting the agricultural endeavors that sustain the local populace.

The district's elevation ranges from 10 to 30 meters above sea level, providing a slight rise from the coastal plains. The landscape transitions from the river-fed agricultural valley in the south to the more urbanized northern edge bordering the Industrial Zone.

## Demographics & Economy

The South District is home to both poor and middle-class residents, with indigenous communities reflecting a strong bond with the region's heritage. The traditional farming communities rely heavily on crop yields, which influence the economic status of many residents.

**Economic Activities:**
- Small-scale agriculture (crops, livestock)
- Local markets and trade
- Artisanal crafts
- Tourism (Old Las Flores historical visits)

**Population Dynamics:**
Younger generations often aspire to explore opportunities in the city, seeking to escape the hardships of rural life. However, a recurring trend sees many returning to their roots, either to invest in land or to resume agricultural practices, underscoring the enduring pull of the land.
