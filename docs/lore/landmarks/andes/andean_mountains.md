# Andean Mountains

> Tags: `#landmark` `#natural` `#mountains` `#outdoor` `#tourism` `#indigenous`

## Overview

**Type:** Natural Landmark / Mountain Range
**Location:** Los Andes District
**Elevation:** 1,500–3,000 meters above sea level
**Key Features:** Rugged terrain, dense forests, diverse wildlife, scenic viewpoints
**Alternative Names:** Los Andes, The Mountains

The Andean Mountains form a dramatic natural barrier rising significantly to the east of Las Flores Free City, creating a breathtaking backdrop that contrasts with the urban and coastal landscapes below. This rugged mountain range serves as both a natural wonder and a recreational paradise, offering diverse ecosystems and outdoor opportunities.

---

## Geography & Natural Features

### Physical Characteristics
- **Elevation Range:** 1,500 to 3,000 meters above sea level
- **Terrain:** Rugged slopes, steep cliffs, mountain streams, and panoramic plateaus
- **Forests:** Dense vegetation with diverse flora and fauna
- **Viewpoints:** Scenic overlooks providing panoramic views of the city and Pacific Ocean
- **Water Sources:** Mountain streams and springs feeding into the Río de las Flores

### Climate Zones
- **Lower Slopes (1,500–2,000m):** Temperate climate with lush vegetation
- **Mid-Elevation (2,000–2,500m):** Cooler temperatures with mixed forests
- **High Altitude (2,500–3,000m):** Alpine conditions with unique high-altitude ecosystems

### Wildlife & Biodiversity
- **Diverse Species:** Home to unique Andean flora and fauna
- **Birdwatching:** Rich avian population including endemic species
- **Conservation Areas:** Protected zones preserving natural habitats
