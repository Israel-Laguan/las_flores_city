# Humanity First — Founding Era (2035–2039)

> Tags: `#humanity_first` `#timeline`

---

## 2035: Arrival and Foundation

### March — Daniel Carter Arrives
- 30-year-old Pioneer from Humanity First's Canada branch
- Small grant and mission to establish presence in Las Flores
- Well-versed in community organization; fluent Spanish
- Goal: understand the city's culture, needs, and political landscape

### April — Listening Tours
- Conducts tours in public squares, marketplaces, and local cafes
- Learns about environmental pollution, lack of public spaces, government corruption
- Builds trust as someone who genuinely cares

### June — Forming Key Alliances
Three initial allies:
- **Elena Ramirez** — local teacher passionate about education reform
- **Javier Torres** — young environmental activist concerned about mining pollution
- **Carlos Lopez** — small business owner, skeptical but intrigued

### August — First Project: Community Garden
- Funded by Humanity First's international branch
- Abandoned lot → fresh vegetables + community gathering space
- Small win builds momentum; more locals take interest

---

## 2036: Gaining Momentum

### January — First Community Council
- Based on Humanity First's decentralized model
- Local volunteers with roles for decision-making, project management, outreach
- **Elena Ramirez** appointed as first Chair

### April — Resource-Sharing Program
- Residents borrow tools and equipment for home repairs, gardening, etc.
- Aligns with "Support and Assistance" value
- Popular among locals, especially those who can't afford tools

### June — Overcoming Cultural Challenges
- Projects losing momentum after initial enthusiasm
- Daniel introduces **"community milestones"** — projects broken into phases with celebrations
- Reinforces value of completion; makes work enjoyable

### September — First Fraud Attempt
- **Mateo Rodriguez** tries to raise funds for fictitious youth program
- Carlos Lopez (verifier) visits and finds nothing; Mateo confesses
- Council implements stricter verification processes

---

## 2037: Growth, Setbacks, and Resilience

### February — Environmental Cleanup Campaign
- Javier Torres leads river cleanup near mining pollution
- First **high-profile project**; gains local media attention
- Mining companies start viewing Humanity First with suspicion

### May — Challenges with Local Corruption
- Officials demand bribes for community center permits
- Council refuses; holds public meetings for social pressure
- After months, officials relent — permits approved without bribes

### October — Educational Workshops
- Elena Ramirez organizes digital literacy, sustainable living, and leadership workshops
- Popular with young people; graduates volunteer to join council

### December — First Independent Project
- Locals independently propose a **recycling program**
- Council approves; Javier mentors them
- **Crucial milestone:** locals initiating projects without external guidance

### Late 2037 — **El Tigre Incident** *(see [Rogue Incidents](rogue_incidents.md))*
- Luis "El Tigre" Peralta joins and creates aggressive "Community Security Initiative"
- Removed in February 2038

---

## 2038: Consolidating Influence

### March — Transparency as Brand
- Council shares financial records openly
- Launches monthly **"Transparency Report"** events
- Strengthens reputation as trustworthy organization

### June — Membership Surge
- Growing popularity brings many new volunteers
- **Subcommittees** formed: health, education, environment
- Daniel steps back; local leaders take more responsibility

### September — Media Coverage
- Well-respected journalist writes feature on Humanity First's impact
- Positive coverage attracts small business donations

### November — Youth Council Created
- Led by **Sofia Alvarez** (age 20)
- Film screenings, art workshops focused on HF values
- Quick following among local students

### December — **Diego & Carla Emerge** *(see [Rogue Incidents](rogue_incidents.md))*
- Ambitious entrepreneurs begin building their own following within HF

---

## 2039: Self-Sufficiency and Daniel's Departure

### January — Community Center Opens
- Library, meeting space, classrooms
- First large-scale project completed **without Daniel's oversight**
- Proves council can manage complex projects independently

### February — Diego & Carla's First Initiative
- Propose "community renovation fund"; secure small grant
- Funds diverted to personal expenses; staged cleanup photos

### March — Training the Next Generation
- Daniel mentors council leaders in long-term planning, conflict resolution
- Introduces them to Humanity First's global network

### June — Daniel's Farewell
- Public farewell event; officially steps down
- Emphasizes council is fully self-sufficient
- Remains available as resource but reiterates local ownership

### June — Early Suspicions about Diego & Carla
- Elena and Javier notice inconsistencies in progress reports
- Diego deflects criticism; creates division in council

### August — Local Leadership Transition
- **Elena Ramirez** formally elected as Chair
- **Javier Torres** as Vice-Chair
- **Sofia Alvarez** as head of Youth Council

### October — International Recognition
- Las Flores featured in Humanity First's annual report
- Recognized as model of successful grassroots organization

### October — Diego & Carla Public Accountability Meeting
- Vague reports; struggle to provide concrete details
- Community begins to doubt their intentions
