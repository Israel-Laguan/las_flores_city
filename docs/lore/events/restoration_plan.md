## Overview

Addressing the lithium hydroxide contamination in Las Flores required a multi-faceted approach involving various strategies to remove contaminants from water sources, restore soil health, and support the recovery of affected ecosystems. The plan was championed by Evelyn Ruthenberg and Yara Rossi after the closure of the trial.

## Water Purification Methods

### Phytoremediation
Using plants to absorb and neutralize contaminants in water. Certain aquatic plants, such as duckweed and water hyacinths, can efficiently remove lithium from water by taking it up through their roots. This was the cornerstone of Evelyn and Yara's restoration project.

### Constructed Wetlands
Building artificial wetlands using a combination of native vegetation, soil, and water to mimic natural wetland ecosystems. These systems can filter out pollutants like lithium hydroxide as water flows through them.

### Activated Charcoal Filtration
Activated charcoal can be used to absorb lithium ions from contaminated water by passing it through specialized filters. Most effective for treating smaller, contained bodies of water.

### Ion Exchange Resins
Specialized resins can replace lithium ions with other cations (e.g., sodium or potassium), effectively removing lithium from water. Requires regular resin replacement and monitoring.

## Soil Remediation Methods

### Soil Washing
A physical treatment process involving the use of water sprays, mechanical agitation, or chemical reagents to remove contaminated soil particles.

### Bioremediation
Using microorganisms, such as bacteria or fungi, to break down contaminants in soil. Selecting organisms that can tolerate high pH levels and absorb lithium ions is crucial.

### Phytoremediation (Soil)
Planting specific species of plants with deep root systems that can take up and accumulate lithium from soil.

## Ecosystem Restoration

### Aquatic Life Reintroduction
Gradually reintroducing native aquatic plants and animals once water quality has improved enough to support them.

### Riparian Habitat Restoration
Restoring vegetation along riverbanks to create a natural buffer zone, stabilize soil, and provide habitat for wildlife.

### Monitoring and Adaptive Management
Continuously assessing the progress of restoration efforts and adjusting strategies as needed to ensure long-term success.
