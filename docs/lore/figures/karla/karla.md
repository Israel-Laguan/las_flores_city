## Overview

Karla was a local farmer and activist who was murdered in February 2053 by Minera Estrella's corporate enforcers. She had been trying to demand the mining company compensate for contaminating her Carinata oil plantation with lithium hydroxide — the waste had escaped from a pipe explosion at the mining facility, reached a river affluent, and contaminated her crop. Before dying, she passed crucial evidence to Evelyn Ruthenberg and asked her to find Nubia.

## Physical Description

Karla was approximately 24 years old at the time of her death. She had dark hair styled in neat braids, and a face that — even in her final moments — radiated defiance despite the pain. She was found wearing a once-vibrant yellow dress, now stained crimson. She was frail in her dying state, with no strength to move her right arm, which had been severed in several places by a long knife.

## The Evidence

Karla had gathered studies demonstrating lithium's effect on Carinata plants — proof that the contamination had been occurring and affecting local agriculture. She carried three items hidden in her left shoe:
1. **A Key** — to a small wooden box in her apartment
2. **A Small Green Pouch** — containing Carinata seeds that had surprisingly resisted the contamination, and money
3. **A Note** — for her sister Nubia, instructing her to take the money and flee the city

## Relationship to Nubia

Karla and Nubia were **sisters** (they shared the same surnames). They lived together in a small apartment in Las Flores, where they had been tenants for a long time. One of them was vegetarian. After Karla's murder, Nubia was also killed in their apartment — the killers searched the place but missed the small wooden box containing the evidence.

## Legacy

Karla's death set Evelyn on the path of investigation that would eventually lead to the exposure of the Great Lithium Leak cover-up. The Carinata seeds she preserved became a key piece of evidence — plants that showed lithium resistance a full year before the public disaster, proving pre-leak contamination had been documented and ignored.
