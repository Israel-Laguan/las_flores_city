# Sofia (Miner)

> Tags: `#figure` `#mining` `#disability` `#resilience`
>
> **Born:** ~2010s (Andean region)
> **Role:** Mineral sorter, later street survivor
> **Status:** Alive (as of 2050s)

## Physical Description
- Hair: Black, worn in a practical braid
- Eyes: Dark brown, alert and observant
- Build: Petite and wiry, walks with a cane
- Skin: Deep brown, weathered from sun and hardship
- Distinguishing features: Twisted leg requiring a cane, worn clothing, always watches people's lips to lip-read, small gold locket with daughter's photo

## Overview
Sofia was born deaf in a small Andean village. She learned to communicate through hand gestures and lip-reading, and despite her disability, she earned a job sorting minerals at the local mine. Her keen eyes and strong work ethic earned her the respect of her coworkers.

## Tragedy
A rockslide in the mine pinned Sofia's leg beneath a boulder, causing irreparable damage. After multiple surgeries, she was left with a twisted leg and a cane. Unable to work, her savings depleted by medical bills, Sofia ended up on the streets of Old Las Flores.

She survived by begging and foraging, her spirit unbroken. Every penny she earned went toward supporting her daughter Ariana, who lived with Sofia's sister in the City District and was studying to become a doctor. Sofia's greatest pride was knowing that her sacrifice was giving Ariana a future.

## Legacy
Sofia became a quiet fixture on the corners of Old Las Flores — a deaf woman in tattered clothes, signing her daughter's name to herself each night as a lullaby of hope. Her story represents the human cost of the mining industry's ruthless efficiency.
