# Evelyn Ruthenberg

> Tags: `#figure` `#whistleblower` `#luz_del_rio` `#lithium` `#justice` `#shadow_investigator` `#carinata`
> 
> **Born:** ~2029
> **Role:** Former Engineer at Luz del Rio, Whistleblower, Shadow Investigator
> **Status:** Active (as of 2065)

## Overview

Evelyn Ruthenberg is a former engineer at Luz del Rio, a small electric company that served Minera Estrella's facilities. After witnessing the cover-up of the Great Lithium Leak, she became the keeper of crucial evidence and a central figure in the fight for justice. What began as a straightforward whistleblower mission evolved into something far more complex: a shadow investigation running parallel to the public restoration effort, blending manipulation, covert intelligence gathering, and a relentless pursuit of those who got away with murder.

## Background

Evelyn graduated from Universidad Nacional de Las Flores in early 2051 with a degree in engineering. She secured a position at Luz del Rio, excited about the opportunities the growing city offered.

## The Great Lithium Leak (August 15, 2052)

A catastrophic failure of high-pressure pipes at Minera Estrella's processing facility released massive quantities of lithium hydroxide into the Río de las Flores. Hundreds of people in downstream communities died. Evelyn witnessed the cover-up firsthand: internal emails showed executives discussing structural issues *before* the leak, and LW Group orchestrated a media campaign to downplay the disaster.

## The Karla Encounter (February 2053)

Evelyn met Karla, a local farmer whose land had been contaminated by the leak. Karla was also searching for her sister Nubia, who had disappeared months earlier. Before Karla died—stabbed while resisting arrest during a protest—she pressed a study into Evelyn's hands. The study, conducted by biologist **Cecilia Pérez**, demonstrated that Carinata plants had shown lithium resistance **a full year before** the public disaster, proving pre-leak contamination had been documented and ignored.

## The Apartment Discovery (September 2053)

Evelyn found Karla and Nubia's apartment. Inside, a small wooden box contained: a copy of Cecilia Pérez's suppressed study, internal emails between Minera Estrella executives discussing dam structural issues, a handful of **Carinata seeds**, and a note in Karla's handwriting: *"Busca tras el detergente, tengo la plata de las primas, te quiero."* The evidence was irrefutable—corporate negligence, cover-up, and silencing of witnesses.

In October 2053, Evelyn investigated but chose not to come forward, fearing for her life.

## The Gang Attack & Exile (March 2055)

In January 2055, the trial of Minera Estrella executives began. Evelyn attempted to present her evidence in March 2055 but was ambushed by three gang members in an alley: *"Soplona, you're coming with us."* Using self-defense taught by her grandfather—*"los bravitos ganan cuando les muestras miedo"*—she fought free, fled through an abandoned park, and escaped onto a metro train. She abandoned her apartment and fled to her family home in the South District Valley, going underground for three years.

## The Verdict (March 2057)

On January 10, 2057, despite overwhelming evidence, Minera Estrella was acquitted on technicalities. Luz del Rio was scapegoated and sued. Evelyn watched the live broadcast from her mother's house. She had sent her evidence anonymously to Luz del Rio's defense team, but it was never used. The realization that the system was rigged—and that her own mother was dying from lithium complications with no justice in sight—radicalized her. She stopped waiting for legal vindication and began planning to operate outside the rules.

## Return to the City (January–February 2058)

On **January 1, 2058**, Evelyn boarded a bus to the City District, carrying the evidence box, her mother's memory, and a cold resolve. She arrived in February, her mother having died from contamination complications shortly before departure. At a nightclub in February 2058, she met **Yara Rossi**. When Evelyn revealed she had proof against Minería Estrella, Yara's guarded demeanor melted. "I will expose those bastards with the truth." A week later, Yara invited Evelyn to her apartment and introduced her to **Dr. Wei Zhang**.

## Partnership with Yara Rossi (2058–2059)

Evelyn shared her evidence with Yara, who helped her petition to reopen the case. Together with Wei Zhang, they devised a phytoremediation restoration plan. Evelyn discovered she had a talent for convincing people to share secrets, developing a manipulative and analytical edge. But she also began cultivating a covert network: tracking mid-level managers, gathering personal intelligence on corrupt officials, and building leverage that could not be dismissed as easily as witness testimony.

She justified it to herself as insurance. But it was also something darker—a desire to understand the enemy so completely that she could dismantle them piece by piece.

## The Judge's House (End of March 2058)

After a court dismissed their appeal, Evelyn, rage boiling, walked to the presiding judge's house. Mistaking her for a LW Group enforcer in the dark, the judge blurted out that he had personally destroyed the papers and wanted jewelry as payment instead of money. Evelyn realized she could pass for a hired gun, that the corruption was casual, performative. The incident cracked open something in her: she could work in the shadows, become the thing they feared.

## Shadow Transformation

Evelyn became a reluctant strategist. She ran two tracks simultaneously:

- **Public track:** Worked with Yara and Wei on the phytoremediation plan, community meetings, permits, fundraising.
- **Shadow track:** Ran background checks on city council members, mapped bribery networks, located witnesses who had been "disappeared," and shared selective intelligence with sympathetic journalists and international observers.

She called it "working in the dark" and told herself it was all for the cause. But there was a thrill in it too—the exhilaration of deception, of being the unknown variable in a rigged game.

## December 2058: A Different Kind of Hope

When Wei Zhang burst into their planning session with news that the Carinata plant's resistance proved earlier contamination, Evelyn saw the scientific proof she had carried for five years finally validated. While Yara and Wei obsessed over test sites and controlled experiments, Evelyn had a parallel realization: every test Wei ran, every data point he collected, was potential evidence—evidence that could not be dismissed as witness testimony or circumstantial links. She committed to supporting their visible work while continuing her shadow investigations in secret.

## February 2059: The Confrontation

Yara confronted Evelyn about **Oscar**, a contact Evelyn had sent to the district archives who had been missing for three days. "Please stop putting yourself in danger," Yara begged. "We should do it the correct way, together!" Evelyn promised to be careful—but meant she would be careful *not* to involve Yara, to cover her tracks, to keep her investigation hidden. She would not abandon the shadow work, but she learned to compartmentalize it from the people she loved.

## Yara's Assassination (July 14, 2059)

Yara was killed returning from an investor meeting. Evelyn identified the body in the morgue. Alone, without family, Yara had left no next of kin. Evelyn cried for the friend and ally she had failed to protect, and for the guilt that told her it should have been her. That guilt turned to cold fury. The corrupt officials, the mining executives, the powerful and greedy—they would not win.

She left the morgue with unshakable resolve. The fight would continue. In the light and in the shadows.

## Personality & Mannerisms

Evelyn is manipulative, analytical, and fiercely dedicated—but also deeply scarred. She laughs rarely, trusts selectively, and carries an undercurrent of guardedness that sharpened after Yara's death. She is good with people, good at making them lower their defenses and give her what she wants. She knows this about herself and does not always like it. Her moral compass points firmly toward justice, but she has crossed lines she will never fully reconcile: deception, blackmail, the occasional threat. She works in the dark because she believes the system burns daylight.

## Legacy

As of 2065, Evelyn Ruthenberg continues to lead the restoration project publicly while operating an extensive shadow intelligence network. She has exposed subordinate officials, relocated endangered witnesses, and leaked damaging documents to international press. She is the keeper of Cecilia Pérez's Carinata seeds, the curator of Karla and Nubia's evidence, and the silent architect of a justice campaign that runs deeper than any courtroom could reach.

## Key Evidence Held

- Internal emails between Minera Estrella executives discussing structural issues before the leak
- Documents showing bribes paid to city council members
- **Cecilia Pérez's original Carinata phytoremediation study** (pre-leak contamination proof)
- **Carinata seeds** recovered from Karla and Nubia's apartment
- Testimony from whistleblowers within Minera Estrella
- A copy of Liu Fang's memory card evidence
- Intelligence files on corrupt officials, judges, and enforcers
