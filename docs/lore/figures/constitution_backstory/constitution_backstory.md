# The Free City Constitution — Backstory

> Tags: `#free_city` `#history` `#juan_pablo_ramos` `#van_der_meer` `#lw_group` `#politics`

## Overview

**Event:** Establishment of Las Flores as a Free City
**Year:** ~2036
**Key Figure:** Juan Pablo Ramos
**Backers:** Van der Meer Family / Global Lithium Corp (GLC)
**Opposition:** LW Group / Chinese state interests

The transformation of Las Flores from a mining settlement into a Free City is the foundational political event of the modern city. It represents the culmination of a power struggle between European and Chinese interests, embodied in the rivalry between the Van der Meer family and the LW Group.

---

## Historical Context

### The Mining Boom (2020s–2030s)
Las Flores began as a small settlement around mining operations. The discovery of massive lithium deposits attracted two competing foreign powers:

- **Chinese Interests (LW Group):** Arrived first, established Minera Estrella, and began large-scale extraction
- **Dutch/European Interests (GLC/Van der Meer):** Discovered a separate, larger vein and established Van der Meer Mining

### Tensions Rise
As both operations expanded, tensions grew over:
- Land rights and mining concessions
- Labor force competition
- Infrastructure development priorities
- Political control of the growing settlement

---

## The Free City Campaign

### Juan Pablo Ramos
A charismatic local leader, Juan Pablo Ramos emerged as the figure who could unite the diverse factions of Las Flores under a single vision: independence from national government control and the establishment of a Free City that could manage its own affairs.

### Van der Meer Backing
The Van der Meer family, through GLC, recognized that a Free City status would:
- Reduce Chinese influence in local governance
- Create a more favorable business environment for European interests
- Allow for independent infrastructure development
- Establish clear property rights and mining concessions

They provided financial backing for Ramos's political campaign, funding:
- Legal and administrative costs for the Free City application
- Public relations and community outreach
- Lobbying efforts with national and international bodies

### LW Group Opposition
The LW Group opposed Free City status, fearing:
- Loss of favorable mining concessions
- Increased regulatory power for local government
- Van der Meer family influence over city administration
- Potential restrictions on Chinese business operations

---

## The Constitution

### Key Provisions
The Free City Constitution established:

1. **Autonomous Governance:** Las Flores would govern itself independently of national government
2. **Free Trade Zone:** Designated areas for duty-free commerce
3. **Property Rights:** Clear legal framework for land ownership and mining concessions
4. **Foreign Investment Protections:** Guarantees for international businesses
5. **Multilingual Status:** Spanish as official language, English and Chinese as secondary official languages
6. **Independent Judiciary:** Courts separate from national system

### The Compromise
The final constitution represented a compromise between the competing factions:
- Mining concessions for both Chinese and Dutch operations were protected
- A balanced city council with representation from various districts
- Provisions for future expansion and development

## Legacy

### Monument
Plaza de la Constitución in the City District features a monument to Juan Pablo Ramos and the Free City status, symbolizing the city's historical journey and identity.

### Ongoing Tensions
The Free City status did not end the rivalry between Chinese and European interests. Instead, it created a new arena — city politics — where the struggle continues through:
- City council elections and lobbying
- Public-private partnership negotiations
- Infrastructure project bidding
- Regulatory battles over mining and environmental standards

### Modern Relevance (2070s)
The Free City constitution remains a living document, referenced in:
- Debates over mining regulations
- Environmental protection disputes
- Labor rights and worker protections
- Criminal jurisdiction (the Flowers Syndicate operates across the Free City's borders)
