## Overview

Isabella Marquez, known to friends and neighbors as Bella, is a vibrant and magnetic figure in Las Flores's port district. A widow-by-disappearance three times over, she is the devoted single mother of Joaquín (10), Mateo (8), and Luis (5). Raised in a small coastal village where family and tradition are paramount, she has carried that warmth into the port's transient community, opening her home to sailors, dockworkers, and neighbors alike.

## Background & Early Life

Bella grew up in a tight-knit coastal village where communal bonds and cultural celebration shaped her identity. She learned to cook, dance, and tell stories alongside generations of relatives and neighbors. When she relocated to Las Flores's bustling port area, she brought that same spirit of hospitality and resilience, quickly becoming a beloved fixture in a world dominated by constant movement and departure.

## Children & Family

Each of Bella's three sons has a different father—sailors from different parts of the world. Every relationship ended in disappearance; no explanation, no goodbye. Rather than retreat, Bella leaned harder into community. Her own mother provides critical stability, caring for the boys when Bella works, attends celebrations, or simply needs a night off.

## Social Life & Nightlife

Bella's warmth and vitality draw people to her like a magnet. She is a regular at port-area bars and local celebrations, where she dances, flirts, and laughs as though each evening is a gift. Casual encounters and fleeting romances are part of her rhythm—not because she is searching for another father for her children, but because she refuses to let grief harden her capacity for joy.

## Personality & Coping

- **Vibrant & Expressive:** Bella is the life of the gathering, known for her laughter, dance moves, and gift for storytelling.
- **Resilient:** Having lost three partners to unexplained disappearances, she has built a life that depends not on one person but on many.
- **Community-Oriented:** Her home is an informal gathering place where neighbors and travelers alike feel welcome.

Bella copes by living fully in the present, teaching her sons to do the same. She fears deeply, however, that the transient world of the port may eventually take one of her children the way it took their fathers.

## Complications

- **Judgment:** More conservative community members raise eyebrows at Bella's lifestyle, worrying about her influence on young boys.
- **Fear of Loss:** Despite her confident exterior, she lives with a quiet terror that history will repeat itself.
- **Financial Pressure:** Raising three children alone in an expensive city requires a support network that she does not take for granted.
