Elisabeth van der Meer, a member of the prominent Dutch Van der Meer family, is known throughout Las Flores as an elegant tastemaker, art collector, and philanthropist. She married Carlos Alberto Hernandez in 2038 in a union intended to cement alliances between the Dutch and local political interests. ## High Society & Philanthropy
When her father Pieter expanded the family's mining interests into Las Flores, Elisabeth moved to the region and quickly became an active, central member of its high society. She was both a participant and a tastemaker, hosting lavish parties at her sprawling riverbank estate. These events invited prominent figures from politics, business, and arts to mingle, solidifying the van der Meers' status as key players in Las Flores while providing opportunities for Elisabeth to indulge in her passion for culture.

Elisabeth is an avid collector of local art, particularly paintings by Shipibo-Konibo artists. Her collection is one of the most extensive in Las Flores, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the region's indigenous people. She frequently loans pieces to local museums, raising public awareness of these artisans' work.

Beyond the arts, Elisabeth serves on the boards of several non-profits focused on education, healthcare, and women's empowerment initiatives. Through these roles, she uses her influence to advocate for policies that improve the lives of Las Flores' residents, particularly marginalized communities.

## Marriage and Tensions
Despite her glittering public life, her private life was fraught with tension. The union with Carlos Alberto Hernandez was troubled from the start. Carlos Hernandez senior's inappropriate behavior toward Elisabeth made her deeply uncomfortable, and his patriarchal expectations clashed with her European, independent upbringing. Meanwhile, Carlos Alberto's emotional dependence on his father frustrated Elisabeth's attempts to build a genuine partnership.

Elisabeth maintained close ties with her Dutch family, whose values of integrity and independence contrasted sharply with the Hernandez clan's corruption. Her visits from Dutch relatives served as a constant reminder of what she had sacrificed in marrying into Las Flores' political elite. She was trapped between her desire for the material comfort of the ELU lifestyle and her disdain for the Chinese business interests and corruption that funded her husband's world. 

## Later Life
After her eventual divorce from Carlos Alberto, Elisabeth focused on raising her daughter, Sophia, and maintaining close ties with her extended family. Even as public scrutiny surrounding her ex-son-in-law (Carlos Rodriguez Jr.) cast shadows over the family name, Elisabeth remained an unwavering presence in Las Flores' social scene, continually using her platform to support causes that matter most to her.
