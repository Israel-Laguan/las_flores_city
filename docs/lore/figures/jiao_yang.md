# Jiao Yang

**Title (full):** Community Advocate and Aspiring Graphic Designer

**Description (full):**

Jiao Yang is a vibrant second-generation Chinese-American born and raised in the Chinatown neighborhood of Las Flores' Northeast district. Growing up between two cultures, she developed a deep appreciation for both her heritage and the opportunities of her American upbringing. After earning a Business Administration degree from a local university, Jiao secured a position as an office assistant at a multinational corporation in the city's financial district—her first step toward building a professional career.

Despite her corporate role, Jiao remains deeply rooted in her community. She actively preserves Chinese culture through practicing traditional dance, occasionally performing at neighborhood festivals and cultural events. Her weekends are often spent exploring new restaurants with friends, sampling both authentic Chinese cuisine and the diverse culinary landscape of Las Flores. Jiao also volunteers regularly at a local community center that supports recent Chinese immigrants, helping them navigate language barriers, job searches, and cultural adjustment.

Ambitious and creative, Jiao is cultivating a growing interest in graphic design. She spends her evenings experimenting with design software, creating digital art that blends traditional Chinese motifs with modern aesthetics—a reflection of her own bicultural identity. While she currently lives with her parents to save for her own place, her ultimate goal is to transform this passion into a side hustle or even a full-time career path.

Personality-wise, Jiao radiates warmth and approachability. Her ready smile and willingness to help make her a natural connector in both professional and social settings. Yet beneath her friendly exterior lies a quiet determination; she advocates fiercely for herself and her community when needed, unafraid to speak up against injustice or unfair treatment. This balance of kindness and conviction makes her a respected figure among her peers—a bridge between cultures, generations, and aspirations in the dynamic tapestry of Las Flores 2077.
