## Overview
Alicia Quevedo became the **second Governor** of Las Flores in 2041, succeeding Juan Pablo Ramos after his two terms (2033–2041). Her administration shifted the Free City from its founding libertarian era toward social democratic policies, focusing on welfare programs and reducing economic inequality caused by the mining boom.

## Tenure
Quevedo's four-year term was part of the transition from the founding coalition era to competitive party politics. Her government laid groundwork for the institutional structures later tested by the Great Lithium Leak crisis.
