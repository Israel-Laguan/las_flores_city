## Overview

Born in 2055 in Beijing, China, Yifan Wang grew up in a household that instilled a deep sense of national pride and cultural identity. In 2075, at age 20, he moved to Las Flores to pursue a degree in International Relations at Universidad del Valle (UDV), one of the city's most prestigious institutions.

## Background

Yifan quickly made his presence felt on campus. Within his first year he ran for president of the UDV Chinese Students Association and won, energizing a previously low-profile organization into a visible force on campus. Under his leadership, the association expanded its programming to include academic workshops, professional networking events, and large-scale cultural showcases. His most ambitious project to date is an upcoming National Day celebration featuring Chinese music, dance, and cuisine — an event he hopes will cement the association's standing and raise awareness of Chinese culture across Las Flores.

## Relationships

He is not shy about challenging university administrators or fellow students when he feels Chinese interests are being sidelined or misrepresented. His arguments are sharp, his delivery charismatic, and his conviction unshakeable — qualities that earn him loyal allies and equally committed critics. He navigates campus politics with the patience and long-game thinking of someone who views UDV as a stepping stone rather than a destination.

Yifan lives in a dormitory reserved for international students from East Asia. His room is cluttered with political theory texts, Mandarin-language news feeds, and planning notes for the association's next event. He keeps a small, framed photo of Tiananmen Square on his desk — a reminder of where he intends to return, eventually, in an official capacity.

## Legacy

His long-term goal is a position in the Chinese foreign service or a major international organization, where he can shape policy at a global scale. Whether his UDV years will be remembered as a launchpad for that career or as the moment his political instincts were first forged on foreign soil remains to be seen.
