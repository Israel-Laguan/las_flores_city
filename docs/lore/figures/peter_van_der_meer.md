## Overview

Peter van der Meer, the charismatic son of Jan and Maria van der Meer, publicly revealed his homosexuality in **2065** in a landmark moment for Las Flores' LGBTQ+ rights movement. His bold announcement marked a turning point for the city and helped catalyze a surge in queer activism.

## Coming Out (2065)

At a time when LGBTQ+ rights were still a contentious issue globally, Peter's public revelation sent shockwaves through the family and the city.

> "My truth has always been that I am gay. I've hidden this part of myself for too long, and it's time to embrace who I am fully."

Flanked by his then-wife Rafaela and their young son Peter Jr., Peter made an emotional press conference announcement.

### Family Reaction
- **Supporters:** Jan Jr., his cousin and husband, was one of his staunchest allies
- **Opposition:** Some family members expressed concern, fearing damage to the van der Meer legacy
- **Public Impact:** Peter's courage inspired queer individuals across Las Flores to demand equal rights

## Marriage of Convenience

Peter married **Rafaela** in a marriage arranged to please their families. They never loved each other romantically but sought to provide a stable home for their son, Peter Jr.

### Rafaela's Tell-All (2070)
In a 2070 interview, Rafaela broke her silence:
> "Peter and I never loved each other romantically. We married to please our families and give Peter Jr. a stable home."

She expressed concern about her son's sexuality:
> "I worry that Peter Jr. will face the same struggles his father did, hiding who he is."

## Legacy and Activism

Despite Rafaela's fears, **Peter Jr.** emerged as a vocal LGBTQ+ advocate, co-founding "Amor Verdadero" (True Love), an organization championing queer causes and supporting families with LGBTQ+ members.

### "Amor Verdadero"
- Founded by Peter Jr. and other young LGBTQ+ leaders
- Mission: Champion queer causes and support families with LGBTQ+ members
- Impact: Helped shape Las Flores' increasingly inclusive social landscape

## Las Flores' LGBTQ+ Rights Progress

Under the van der Meers' influence, Las Flores made significant strides toward equality:
- **2075:** City passed landmark legislation outlawing discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity
- This marked a historic moment for the once-conservative mining town

## Family Context

Peter's coming out and subsequent activism represent one branch of the sprawling van der Meer family tree. His parents Jan and Maria, cousin and husband Jan Jr., son Peter Jr., and ex-wife Rafaela form a family unit whose personal dynamics have shaped not only the family's public image but also Las Flores' social progress.
