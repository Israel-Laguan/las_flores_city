Ana Ramirez is a passionate indigenous political leader who heads La Coalición por la Justicia Social (CJS). She champions the rights of marginalized communities and fights against environmental destruction caused by unchecked mining expansion in Las Flores.

---

## Background

Ana was born into a family of indigenous activists in Las Flores' rural outskirts. From an early age, she witnessed firsthand the impact of mining activities on native communities and their ancestral lands. These early experiences shaped her worldview and inspired her to pursue a degree in environmental science.

After completing her education, Ana dedicated herself to protecting both the environment and indigenous rights, founding La Coalición por la Justicia Social (CJS) as a platform for advocacy and community empowerment.

---

## Organization & Leadership

As the founder and leader of CJS, Ana crafts effective strategies for advocacy and education. The organization works closely with progressive politicians and other grassroots initiatives to raise awareness about the environmental and social costs of unchecked mining expansion.

CJS operates through nonviolent direct action, grassroots organizing, and educational campaigns that center the experiences of native communities. Ana maintains strong ties with progressive politicians who share her commitment to indigenous rights and environmental protection.
