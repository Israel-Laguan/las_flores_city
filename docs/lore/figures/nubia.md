# Nubia

> **Died:** February 2053

## Overview

Nubia was the **sister of Karla** — they shared the same surnames and lived together in a small apartment in Las Flores. Both were murdered in February 2053 in what authorities treated as a common robbery, though those close to the case suspect a targeted silencing connected to the lithium contamination evidence. Evelyn Ruthenberg discovered their apartment and the suppressed evidence they had been hiding in September 2053.

## The Apartment

Karla and Nubia lived in a small apartment in Las Flores, where they had been tenants for a long time. When Evelyn investigated months after the murders, she found:
- **Photos and bills** with both their names, confirming they were sisters
- **Food still in the fridge**, the bed made, trash in the bathroom — signs of a sudden departure
- Signs that **one of them was vegetarian**
- A **small wooden box** that the killers had missed during their search

## The Evidence

The small wooden box, unlocked by the key Karla had given Evelyn in her dying moments, contained:
- A copy of **Cecilia Pérez's suppressed study** showing lithium resistance a year before the public disaster
- **Internal emails** between Minera Estrella executives discussing structural issues before the leak
- A handful of **Carinata seeds** — plants that had surprisingly resisted the contamination
- **Bills and documents** showing their long tenancy

The killers had searched the apartment but missed the box. Nubia had been killed in the apartment itself — unlike Karla, who died in the alley.

## Legacy

Nubia's death, alongside Karla's, provided Evelyn with the evidence she needed to understand the scope of the Minera Estrella cover-up. The sisters' sacrifice — their attempt to document and expose the contamination — became the foundation of Evelyn's two-decade campaign for justice.
