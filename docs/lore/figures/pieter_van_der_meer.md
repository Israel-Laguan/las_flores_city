# Pieter van der Meer

>
> **Born:** ~1980s (Netherlands)
> **Role:** Dutch consortium leader, Global Lithium Corp
> **Status:** Active

## Overview
Pieter van der Meer led the Dutch mining consortium that discovered the lithium vein near Las Flores in 2029. A strategic thinker, he recognized early that Chinese dominance of the port and mining operations threatened Dutch interests. He orchestrated the "Free City" initiative to equalize power and secure Dutch access to the city's resources.

## The Free City Scheme
Pieter approached local businessman Juan Pablo Ramos with a plan: create a new political entity — a "free city" — that would attract foreign investment and dilute Chinese control. Through a combination of persuasion, bribery, and political maneuvering, Pieter built a coalition that included Mayor Hernandez and key local officials.

The negotiations with the Chinese were intense. Pieter secured crucial concessions: the Dutch gained control of the airport and the southern part of the city, while the Chinese kept the port and the north. The Free City Constitution passed in 2033, and Juan Pablo Ramos became the first Governor.

## Philosophy
Pieter believes the lithium wealth of Las Flores should benefit Dutch and Latin American interests, not be hoarded by Chinese state-linked conglomerates. He is pragmatic, ruthless in negotiation, and convinced that his actions serve the greater good of the region.
