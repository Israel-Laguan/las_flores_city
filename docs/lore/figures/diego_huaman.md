# Diego Huamán

**Title (full):** Environmental Science Student and Community Advocate
**Title (short):** Diego Huamán, Environmental Science Major

**Description (full):**
**Age:** 19
**Origin:** Andean Mountains surrounding Las Flores
**Occupation:** University Student

Diego Huamán is a first-generation university student from a small Andean village outside Las Flores. At 19, he is pursuing a degree in Environmental Science at the Central Las Flores campus, balancing the weight of his family's expectations with the excitement of new opportunities.

Physically, Diego is lean and athletic—built from years of hiking steep mountain trails and working his family's potato and quinoa fields. He has dark, curly hair and warm brown eyes. He dresses practically, moving easily between boots and jeans for farm work and the casual urban style of the city. His most prized possession is a handwoven mochila, made by artisans in his village, which he carries everywhere.

Diego's personality is shaped by his upbringing: ambitious and curious, yet deeply rooted in Andean values of community and respect. He is resilient, having left his village for the city, and has quickly made friends by sharing stories of mountain life. He is passionate about wildlife conservation and plays the charango, a small Andean stringed instrument, whenever he returns home for festivals and gatherings.

Challenges haunt him, too. The pace of city life is faster than anything he experienced in the village, and he struggles with homesickness. His family's modest means mean he works part-time at a local café to cover expenses, juggling shifts with a demanding course load. Financial constraints often keep him awake at night, but he channels these worries into his studies.

Diego's larger vision is to return to his community after graduation with practical knowledge in sustainable farming and environmental protection. He hopes to establish a cooperative that helps local farmers implement eco-friendly practices while preserving their cultural heritage. For Diego, success is not just personal—it is collective.
