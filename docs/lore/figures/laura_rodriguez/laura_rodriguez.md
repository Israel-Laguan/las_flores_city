# Laura Rodríguez

**Age:** 48

**Gender:** Female

**Occupation:** Human Rights Lawyer, Founder & Director of COFAVIC (Comité de Familiares Víctimas del Conflicto)

**Physical Description:**
- Hair: Dark brown, streaked with gray, worn in a practical bun
- Eyes: Dark brown, intense and compassionate
- Build: Medium height, slender but strong, carries herself with quiet authority
- Skin: Medium brown, lined around eyes from stress and long hours
- Distinguishing features: Small reading glasses on a chain, simple gold earrings, always carries a worn leather briefcase, thin scar on left hand from a protest incident

**Background:**
Laura was born and raised in a working-class neighborhood of Las Flores. Her life’s work began when her younger brother went missing after being caught in the crossfire between criminal organizations and security forces. The trauma of his disappearance sparked her passion for advocating on behalf of victims’ families. She earned her law degree at a prestigious university in the capital, specializing in human rights and international law.

**Personality Traits:**
- Tenacious: Laura’s unwavering commitment to justice drives her to pursue even the most complex and challenging cases, often in the face of resistance from powerful interests.
- Empathetic: Deeply moved by the suffering of others, she approaches each case with compassion, empathy, and a genuine desire to help those in need.
- Strategic: As both a lawyer and an activist, Laura possesses a keen ability to craft effective strategies for advocacy, litigation, and community mobilization.

**Motivations:**
Laura believes, "No one should have to live in fear or suffer the loss of loved ones due to violence and impunity. It’s my mission to seek justice for the victims and their families." She aims to hold perpetrators accountable, support victims’ rights, and promote a culture of peace and justice in Las Flores.

**Relationships & Alliances:**
- Founded COFAVIC (Comité de Familiares Víctimas del Conflicto) to provide legal aid, advocacy, and emotional support for families affected by Las Flores’ ongoing problems.
- Maintains close ties with other human-rights activists, including Juan Carlos Pérez, Ana Ramírez, Martín López, and María Fernanda Rodríguez, collaborating on cases that intersect with their respective causes.
- Regularly partners with international organizations like Human Rights Watch and Amnesty International to pressure the Las Flores government into action.

**Mannerisms:**
- Speaks passionately about the importance of human rights and justice during public appearances and interviews.
- Maintains a strong commitment to listening to and amplifying the voices of victims and their families, always prioritizing their needs and concerns.
- Works tirelessly behind the scenes, often taking on multiple cases at once and devoting herself fully to her mission.
