## Overview
Elisabeth van der Meer, a member of the prominent Dutch Van der Meer family, married Carlos Alberto Hernandez in 2038 in a union intended to cement alliances between the Dutch and local political interests. The marriage was a turning point for both families — and for Las Flores politics.

## Marriage and Tensions
The union was troubled from the start. Carlos Hernandez senior's inappropriate behavior toward Elisabeth made her deeply uncomfortable. His patriarchal expectations clashed with her European, independent upbringing. Meanwhile, Carlos Alberto's emotional dependence on his father frustrated Elisabeth's attempts to build a genuine partnership.

Elisabeth maintained close ties with her Dutch family, whose values of integrity and independence contrasted sharply with the Hernandez clan's corruption. Her visits from Dutch relatives served as a constant reminder of what she had sacrificed in marrying into Las Flores' political elite.

## Complications
Elisabeth initially enjoyed the luxury that came with the ELU lifestyle, but grew increasingly uncomfortable with the Chinese business interests that dominated her husband's world. She is trapped between desire for material comfort and disdain for the corruption that funds it.
