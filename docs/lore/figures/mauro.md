# Mauro (Kusi)

> **Born:** ~1970s (Andean region)
> **Role:** Bus driver, "La Diabla"
> **Status:** Active

## Physical Description
- Hair: Gray and thinning, cropped short and rough
- Eyes: Dark brown, tired but kind
- Build: Stocky and weathered, bent slightly from years of mining
- Skin: Deep brown, heavily lined and rough from sun and labor
- Distinguishing features: Calloused scarred hands, traditional woven cap, faded work clothes, quiet melancholic smile

## Overview
Mauro (also known as Kusi — which means "joy" or "happiness" in Quechua) is a grizzled Quechua elder who spent most of his life working in the Andean mines. When the Chinese investors phased out older indigenous workers, he retrained as a bus driver in Old Las Flores.

His bus, nicknamed "La Diabla" (The Devil), was notorious for its deafening sound system. Mauro endured the cacophony because the job paid the bills, and he took pride in knowing the city's winding streets like the back of his hand.

## The Incident
One fateful day, Mauro's attention wavered. He failed to notice a little girl stepping off the curb, and La Diabla struck her, causing a serious leg injury. The incident devastated him. The settlement was hefty, and the girl's pained expression haunted him for years.

## Redemption
After the accident, Mauro turned down the music — permanently. La Diabla became the only bus in town where passengers could actually think. His passengers appreciated the quiet, and Mauro's renewed vigilance behind the wheel prevented further incidents. He whistled traditional Quechua melodies to himself as he drove, a gentle penance for his moment of distraction.
