## Career Arc

### 1. Translator & Local Guide (2024–2025)

In her early twenties, Elena Torres leveraged her bilingual upbringing (Spanish-Mandarin) to serve as a translator and local guide for **China Lithium & Metals (CLM)** during their initial exploratory drilling phase in Las Flores. Her fluency in Mandarin and deep knowledge of local geography and culture made her indispensable to both the Chinese engineers and the municipal authorities they negotiated with. She helped coordinate environmental impact assessments and community consultations — work that gave her early exposure to the tension between mining interests and local environmental concerns.

### 2. Environmental Science Studies (2025–2030)

After her time with CLM, Elena pursued formal education in **environmental science** at the National University, specializing in mining impact assessment and remediation. During her studies she remained engaged with Las Flores' industrial sector, conducting independent research on groundwater contamination patterns near active mining sites. Her graduate work earned her recognition from both environmental NGOs and corporate sustainability offices.

### 3. Media Relations & Crisis PR (2052)

When the Great Lithium Leak occurred in August 2052, Elena was recruited by **Li Wei** himself to lead the company's media response. Her environmental science credentials gave her apparent credibility, and she was tasked with orchestrating the narrative that minimized the disaster's severity. She proposed a comprehensive cleanup plan but also confirmed to Li Wei in the emergency meeting that long-term damage was inevitable. Her involvement in the cover-up — framing the disaster as an electrical failure and controlling media narratives — was later revealed during the 2055 trial.

### 4. Sustainability Lead (by 2077)

Elena's career within LW Group continued to rise. By 2077, she had become the company's **Sustainability Lead**, managing community engagement, environmental compliance reporting, and corporate social responsibility initiatives. In this role she represents LW Group's public-facing reform effort — building schools, funding restoration projects, and negotiating with community leaders. Her arc from young translator to crisis PR manager to sustainability executive spans five decades and mirrors the transformation of Las Flores itself.

## ✅ RESOLVED — Career Arc
The apparently contradictory roles of "young translator" (2024–2025), "crisis PR manager" (2052), and "corporate sustainability executive" (2077) are reconciled as sequential phases of one person's career. Elena Torres was present at the founding of CLM's Las Flores operations and remained embedded in the LW Group ecosystem throughout the city's transformation.
