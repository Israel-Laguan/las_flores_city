# Tax System of the Free City of Las Flores

> Tags: `#governance` `#taxes` `#economy` `#fiscal`

## Overview

The Free City of Las Flores operates a progressive tax system designed to promote economic growth while generating revenue from luxury goods and high-income earners. The system exempts basic necessities and supports low-income families to address inequality without overly penalizing wealth and investment.

## For Individuals (Natural Persons)

### Progressive Income Tax Rates

| Income Bracket | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to $25,000 | 0% |
| $25,001 to $50,000 | 5% |
| $50,001 to $100,000 | 10% |
| $100,001 to $500,000 | 15% |
| Above $500,001 | 20% |

### Sales Tax

A general sales tax of 5% on most goods and services, with exemptions for basic necessities like food and medicine.

### Property Tax

- For primary residences: 0.5% of the property's assessed value per year.
- For secondary or luxury properties: 1.5% of the property's assessed value per year.

---

## For Companies (Judicial Persons)

### Corporate Tax Rate

| Annual Revenue Bracket | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to $500,000 | 5% |
| $500,001 to $1,000,000 | 10% |
| $1,000,001 to $5,000,000 | 15% |
| Above $5,000,001 | 20% |

### Special Economic Zones

Companies operating in designated economic zones focused on exports or high-tech industries can receive tax holidays for the first 5 years of operation, followed by a 50% reduction in the corporate tax rate for the subsequent 5 years.

### Import and Export Taxes

**Import Taxes:**
| Category | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Basic necessities and raw materials for manufacturing | 0% |
| General goods | 5% |
| Luxury goods | 20% |

**Export Taxes:**
| Category | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Agricultural goods and raw materials | 0% |
| Manufactured goods | 2% |
| Luxury and high-end products | 10% |

---

## Additional Social Support Measures

### Subsidized Housing and Healthcare

Government-funded programs to provide affordable housing and healthcare to low-income families and individuals.

This tax model aims to promote economic growth by keeping taxes low for most individuals and businesses while generating revenue from luxury goods and high-income earners. The focus on exempting basic necessities and supporting low-income families helps to address inequality without overly penalizing wealth and investment.
