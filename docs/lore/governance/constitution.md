# Constitution of the Free City of Las Flores

> Tags: `#governance` `#constitution` `#free_city` `#law`

## Overview

The Constitution of the Free City of Las Flores was ratified in 2036 after a controversial referendum with 65% voter approval. It establishes the legal and political framework for the Free City's self-governance, defining the relationship between the Free City and the national government, citizenship, taxation, legal jurisdiction, and the balance of powers.

## Article I: Autonomy and Jurisdiction

**Autonomy:** The Free City of Las Flores is a self-governing entity within the National territory, with the authority to legislate and administer its internal affairs as outlined in this Constitution.

**Jurisdiction:** The jurisdiction of Las Flores extends to all matters not explicitly reserved for the national government, including but not limited to local governance, economic policy, public services, and citizenship.

---

## Article II: Citizenship and Immigration

**Eligibility:** The Free City of Las Flores may grant citizenship to individuals who meet criteria established by the City Council, irrespective of their national origin or current citizenship status.

**Process:** The Citizenship and Immigration Department of Las Flores shall oversee the naturalization process, which includes application, residency requirements, and a commitment to the principles and values of the Free City.

**Rights and Obligations:** Citizens of Las Flores are entitled to all rights and subject to all obligations outlined in this Constitution, including the right to vote, hold public office, and access public services.

---

## Article III: Taxation and Fiscal Policy

**Authority:** The Free City of Las Flores has the authority to levy and collect taxes within its jurisdiction, manage its finances, and determine its fiscal policies.

**Coordination with National Taxation:** Citizens of Las Flores, including those naturalized under Article II, shall be subject to national taxation as per the National laws. The Free City shall cooperate with national tax authorities to ensure compliance.

---

## Article IV: Relationship with the National Government

**Representation:** The Free City of Las Flores shall have representation in the national legislature as determined by national law.

**Coordination:** The Free City shall coordinate with the national government on matters of shared interest, including but not limited to security, environmental protection, and major infrastructure projects.

**Dispute Resolution:** Any disputes between the Free City and the national government shall be resolved through negotiation or, if necessary, arbitration by a body agreed upon by both parties.

---

## Article V: Military and Defense

**Limitations:** The Free City of Las Flores shall maintain a limited military presence, with primary responsibility for defense and security resting with the national government.

**Cooperation:** The Free City will cooperate with the national government on defense matters, providing assistance as required for the maintenance of national security.

---

## Article VI: Legal Jurisdiction

**Primary Jurisdiction:** The Free City of Las Flores shall have primary legal jurisdiction over all matters within its territory, except where explicitly reserved for the national government.

**Conflict Resolution:** Legal conflicts between the Free City and the national government shall be resolved through a mutually agreed judicial process or arbitration body.

---

## Article VII: Law Enforcement and Police Jurisdiction

**Local Policing:** The Free City of Las Flores shall establish and maintain a police force responsible for law enforcement within its jurisdiction.

**Cooperation with National Authorities:** The Free City's police force shall collaborate with national law enforcement authorities on issues of shared concern, including cross-border crime and security threats.

---

## Article VIII: Land Ownership and Property Rights

**Ownership:** Land within the Free City may be owned by individuals, corporations, or the state, subject to the laws and regulations established by the City Council.

**Protection of Property Rights:** The Free City guarantees the protection of property rights, ensuring fair compensation in cases of eminent domain or public use.

---

## Article IX: Protection of Indigenous Communities

**Recognition:** The Free City recognizes and respects the rights of indigenous communities within its territory, including their traditional lands, cultures, and ways of life.

**Consultation and Participation:** The Free City shall ensure the inclusion of indigenous communities in decisions affecting their interests, through consultation processes and participation mechanisms.

---

## Article X: National Intervention and Revocation of Rights

**Conditions:** In exceptional circumstances, where the Free City's actions pose a significant threat to national security or violate fundamental principles of human rights, The Nation reserves the right to intervene within the Free City's jurisdiction.

**Revocation of Rights:** In cases where the Free City's autonomy is revoked, the rights of its citizens may be temporarily suspended, pending resolution of the situation through legal and constitutional means.

**Legal Process:** Any intervention or revocation of rights must follow due process of law, ensuring transparency, accountability, and respect for the principles of this Constitution.

---

## Article XI: Legal System

**Foundation:** The legal system of Las Flores is grounded in the principles of fairness, transparency, and the rule of law.

**Courts:** The Free City shall establish and maintain a system of courts, with the Supreme Court of Las Flores as the highest judicial authority within its jurisdiction.

---

## Article XII: Administrative Structure and Power Balances

**Governor:** The executive power in the Free City shall be vested in the Governor, elected directly by the citizens for a term of 4 years.

**City Council:** Legislative authority is entrusted to the City Council, consisting of representatives elected by the districts, ensuring equitable representation.

**Districts:** The Free City is divided into administrative districts, each with a degree of local autonomy, managed by District Councils.

**Checks and Balances:** The Governor, City Council, and District Councils operate within a system of checks and balances, ensuring no single entity can monopolize power.

---

## Article XIII: Public-Private Contracts and Special Companies

**Authorization:** The Free City of Las Flores may enter into public-private contracts and establish special companies to execute specific projects or provide services of public interest.

**Regulation:** Such contracts and companies shall be regulated by laws enacted by the City Council, ensuring transparency, competitive practices, and accountability.

**Oversight:** An independent Public-Private Partnership Oversight Committee shall be established to monitor and audit these contracts and companies, reporting to the City Council and the public.
