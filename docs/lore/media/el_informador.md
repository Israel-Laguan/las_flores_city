# El Informador de Las Flores

> Tags: `#media` `#journalism` `#social_justice`

## Overview

**Type:** Newspaper & Digital Platform
**Circulation:** 20,000 (print) + 300,000 (online)
**Staff:** 40 journalists and support staff
**Editor-in-Chief:** Juan Rodríguez
**Location:** Industrial section of the city
**Audience:** Indigenous, Latino, Black communities

El Informador de Las Flores is a small yet influential newspaper and digital platform that caters to Las Flores' marginalized communities. It has long been a voice for the disenfranchised.

---

## Mission

Inform, empower, and connect communities through balanced, accurate, and engaging journalism.

### Content Coverage
- Local news and politics
- Social issues and human rights
- Community events and cultural heritage
- Economic development impact on marginalized communities
- Investigative pieces exposing corruption, inequality, and injustice

---

## Community Reception

| Community | Relationship |
|---|---|
| **Indigenous** | Deep roots, trusted source. Juan Rodríguez's personal commitment earned loyalty |
| **Latino** | Appreciates focus on their stories, cultures, and concerns |
| **Black** | Smaller community feels represented and amplified |
| **Other Residents** | Critical voice advocating against policies that disproportionately benefit foreign investors |

---

## Impact

Despite its relatively small size, El Informador **punches well above its weight**:
- Serves as a critical check on power
- Champions the marginalized
- Respected by community leaders, activists, and citizens alike
- Fosters dialogue, promotes understanding, and advocates for social justice
